[MODELER]:
Model

- Decision variables:
  - M = number of math workbooks produced (integer, ≥0)
  - E = number of English workbooks produced (integer, ≥0)

- Objective:
  Maximize profit P = 15 M + 17 E

- Constraints:
  1. Demand minima: M ≥ 40
  2. Demand minima: E ≥ 60
  3. Production maxima: M ≤ 140
  4. Production maxima: E ≤ 170
  5. Contract: M + E ≥ 200

- Variable domain: M, E ∈ Z (integers). (You may relax to continuous variables; the LP optimum will be integer here.)

Solution reasoning
All profit coefficients are positive, so profit increases with either M or E. Subject to upper bounds, the profit is maximized by producing the maximum allowed of both products. The feasible region is the rectangle [40,140] × [60,170] intersected with the half-space M+E ≥ 200; the corner (M,E) = (140,170) is feasible and gives the maximum profit.

Optimal solution
- M = 140 math workbooks
- E = 170 English workbooks
- Maximum profit = 15·140 + 17·170 = 2,100 + 2,890 = $4,990

Suggested Python libraries to solve
- PuLP (easy-to-use LP/MIP modeling)
- OR-Tools (CP-SAT and linear solvers)
- scipy.optimize.linprog (for continuous LPs)
- cvxpy (convex optimization modeling)

Example (PuLP) snippet
from pulp import LpMaximize, LpProblem, LpVariable, lpSum, LpInteger

prob = LpProblem("Workbooks", LpMaximize)
M = LpVariable("M", lowBound=40, upBound=140, cat=LpInteger)
E = LpVariable("E", lowBound=60, upBound=170, cat=LpInteger)
prob += 15*M + 17*E
prob += M + E >= 200
prob.solve()
print(M.varValue, E.varValue, "Profit =", 15*M.varValue + 17*E.varValue)

This will return M=140, E=170, Profit=4990.

[PROGRAMMER-VALIDATOR]:
# Self-contained script to solve the integer LP by enumeration (no external libraries needed)

# Problem data
coef_M = 15
coef_E = 17
M_min, M_max = 40, 140
E_min, E_max = 60, 170

# Constraints: M + E >= 200
best_profit = None
best_M = None
best_E = None

for M in range(M_min, M_max + 1):
    for E in range(E_min, E_max + 1):
        if M + E < 200:
            continue
        profit = coef_M * M + coef_E * E
        if (best_profit is None) or (profit > best_profit):
            best_profit = profit
            best_M = M
            best_E = E

# At this point best_profit should be the maximum integer profit
# Print final objective value in the required format
print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {best_profit}")
