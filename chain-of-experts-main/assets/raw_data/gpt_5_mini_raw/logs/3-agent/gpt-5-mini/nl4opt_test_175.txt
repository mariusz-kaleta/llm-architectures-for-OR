Formal model

Decision variables
- v ∈ Z_{\ge 0}: number of van trips
- t ∈ Z_{\ge 0}: number of truck trips

Objective
- Minimize total trips: minimize z = v + t

Constraints
1. Capacity: 50 v + 80 t ≥ 1500 (must transport at least 1500 boxes)
2. Budget: 30 v + 50 t ≤ 1000 (budget constraint)
3. Advertising (strict): v − t ≥ 1 (vans make strictly more trips than trucks)
4. Integrality and nonnegativity: v, t ∈ Z_{\ge 0}

(If a solver requires non-strict inequality, v ≥ t + 1 is equivalent to v − t ≥ 1.)

Solution (small integer search or integer programming)
- The integer solutions that satisfy all constraints and minimize v + t have total trips z = 24.
- There are two optimal combinations:
  - v = 14 van trips and t = 10 truck trips, or
  - v = 13 van trips and t = 11 truck trips.
Both satisfy 50v + 80t ≥ 1500 and 30v + 50t ≤ 1000, and v > t.

Suggested solvers / Python libraries
- pulp (with CBC) — easy to model MILP
- OR-Tools CP-SAT or MIP solver — robust and fast
- pyomo (with a MILP solver like CBC, Gurobi, CPLEX)
- pure enumeration (simple double loop) is also trivial here because variables ranges are small

# Integer enumeration solver for the given MILP
# Decision variables: v, t (nonnegative integers)
# Constraints:
#   50*v + 80*t >= 1500
#   30*v + 50*t <= 1000
#   v - t >= 1
# Objective: minimize z = v + t

capacity_needed = 1500
budget_limit = 1000

# Upper bounds from budget constraint to limit search
v_max = budget_limit // 30  # maximum v given budget alone
t_max = budget_limit // 50  # maximum t given budget alone

best_z = None
best_solutions = []

for v in range(0, v_max + 1):
    for t in range(0, t_max + 1):
        if v - t < 1:
            continue
        if 50 * v + 80 * t < capacity_needed:
            continue
        if 30 * v + 50 * t > budget_limit:
            continue
        z = v + t
        if (best_z is None) or (z < best_z):
            best_z = z
            best_solutions = [(v, t)]
        elif z == best_z:
            best_solutions.append((v, t))
