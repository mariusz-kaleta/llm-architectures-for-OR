Decision variables
- x_L ≥ 0 : number of tanks of light oil to process (can be fractional)
- x_N ≥ 0 : number of tanks of non-sticky oil to process
- x_H ≥ 0 : number of tanks of heavy oil to process

Objective
Maximize total net revenue
Max z = 550 x_L + 750 x_N + 950 x_H

Resource constraints
- Compound A: 3 x_L + 6 x_N + 9 x_H ≤ 250
- Compound B: 3 x_L + 2 x_N + 3 x_H ≤ 150

Nonnegativity
x_L, x_N, x_H ≥ 0

(If tanks were required to be integer, add x_L,x_N,x_H ∈ Z≥0; the problem statement allows full or partial tanks so continuous variables are appropriate.)

Solving (analytical)
This is a linear program. At optimum at most two variables are positive because there are two binding constraints. Solving the system with x_H = 0 and both resource constraints binding:

3 x_L + 6 x_N = 250
3 x_L + 2 x_N = 150

Subtracting gives 4 x_N = 100 → x_N = 25
Then 3 x_L + 2·25 = 150 → 3 x_L = 100 → x_L = 100/3 ≈ 33.333...

x_H = 0.

Maximum revenue
z* = 550·(100/3) + 750·25 = 37,083.33 (dollars)

So process about 33.33 tanks of light oil, 25 tanks of non-sticky oil, and 0 tanks of heavy oil to maximize revenue ($37,083.33). Both compound A and B are fully used.
