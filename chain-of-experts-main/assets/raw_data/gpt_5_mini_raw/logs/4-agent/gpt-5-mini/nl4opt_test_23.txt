  - Choose the (V,M) pair with the smallest pollution. This is trivial to implement and guarantees an optimal integer solution.

Objective

Constraints

3. Integrality and bounds:

Complete ILP
Minimise 7V + 10M
subject to
  6V + 10M ≥ 150
  V − M ≥ 1
  0 ≤ M ≤ 10, M ∈ Z
  0 ≤ V ≤ 25, V ∈ Z

Practical/guaranteed-simple solution method
- Enumerate M = 0..10.
- For each M compute required vans:
    V_req_capacity = ceil(max(0, 150 − 10M) / 6)
    V = max(V_req_capacity, M + 1)
  (discard if V > 25)
- Compute P = 7V + 10M and pick the (V,M) with minimum P.

  - V = 10 vans

- This is the minimum; no other (V,M) feasible with M ≤ 10 attains lower pollution.

[CODER]:
import math

# Problem data
CAPACITY_REQUIRED = 150
MAX_M = 10
MAX_V = 25

best_solution = None  # tuple (P, V, M)

for M in range(0, MAX_M + 1):
    # seats provided by minibuses: 10*M
    remaining = max(0, CAPACITY_REQUIRED - 10 * M)
    V_req_capacity = math.ceil(remaining / 6) if remaining > 0 else 0
    V = max(V_req_capacity, M + 1)  # vans must exceed minibuses by at least 1
    if V > MAX_V:
        continue
    P = 7 * V + 10 * M
    if (best_solution is None) or (P < best_solution[0]):
        best_solution = (P, V, M)

if best_solution is None:
    raise ValueError("No feasible solution found within bounds.")

best_P, best_V, best_M = best_solution

# (Optional) print chosen decision variables and seats (not required)
print(f"Chosen solution: V={best_V}, M={best_M}, Seats={6*best_V + 10*best_M}")

# Final required print (must be the last line)
print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {best_P}")
