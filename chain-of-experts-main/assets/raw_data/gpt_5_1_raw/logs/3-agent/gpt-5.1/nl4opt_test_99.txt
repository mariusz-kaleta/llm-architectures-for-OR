[MODELER]:
Define decision variables:

- \(x\): number of small teams  
- \(y\): number of large teams  

Both must be nonnegative integers.

---

### Objective

Maximize the total lawn area mowed:

- Each small team mows 50 sq ft  
- Each large team mows 80 sq ft  

Objective function:
\[
\max \; Z = 50x + 80y
\]

---

### Constraints

1. **Employee availability**

Each small team uses 3 employees; each large team uses 5 employees. Total employees available: 150.

\[
3x + 5y \le 150
\]

2. **Small teams at least three times large teams**

\[
x \ge 3y
\]

3. **Minimum number of large teams**

\[
y \ge 6
\]

4. **Minimum number of small teams**

\[
x \ge 10
\]

5. **Nonnegativity and integrality**

\[
x, y \ge 0,\quad x, y \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

---

### Solving the model (by inspection)

Use the employee constraint as binding to maximize area (more teams use more employees → more lawn):

\[
3x + 5y = 150
\]

Together with \(x = 3y\) (since making \(x\) as small as allowed by \(x \ge 3y\) frees employees for more large teams, which mow more area per employee):

Substitute \(x = 3y\) into \(3x + 5y = 150\):

\[
3(3y) + 5y = 150 \Rightarrow 9y + 5y = 150 \Rightarrow 14y = 150 \Rightarrow y = \frac{150}{14} \notin \mathbb{Z}
\]

Check integer values of \(y\) near \(150/14 \approx 10.71\), respecting all constraints and keeping \(x = 3y\) (tight):

- Try \(y = 10\):  
  - \(x = 3y = 30\)  
  - Employees used: \(3\cdot 30 + 5\cdot 10 = 90 + 50 = 140 \le 150\)  
  - Lawn mowed: \(50\cdot 30 + 80\cdot 10 = 1500 + 800 = 2300\)

- Try \(y = 11\):  
  - \(x = 3y = 33\)  
  - Employees used: \(3\cdot 33 + 5\cdot 11 = 99 + 55 = 154 > 150\) (infeasible)

So \(y\) cannot exceed 10 if \(x = 3y\).

We should also use any remaining employees, if possible, to add more small teams (since large teams require 5 employees and may violate the employee constraint).

At \(x=30, y=10\), employees used = 140, 10 employees left:

- Each additional small team uses 3 employees: max extra small teams = \(\lfloor 10/3 \rfloor = 3\)
- But adding small teams must still satisfy \(x \ge 3y\). With \(y=10\), \(x\) must be at least 30; any extra small teams keep constraint valid.

So new candidate:

- \(x = 30 + 3 = 33\), \(y = 10\)  
- Employees: \(3\cdot 33 + 5\cdot 10 = 99 + 50 = 149 \le 150\)  
- Lawn mowed: \(50\cdot 33 + 80\cdot 10 = 1650 + 800 = 2450\)

Try increasing \(y\) by 1 from there (just to be thorough):

For \(y = 11\), we saw the minimum feasible \(x\) (3y = 33) already exceeds employees. So no feasible pair with \(y \ge 11\).

Now check if reducing \(y\) and adding more \(x\) could help. Note that per employee efficiency:

- Small team: \(50/3 \approx 16.67\) sq ft per employee  
- Large team: \(80/5 = 16\) sq ft per employee  

So small teams are more efficient. However, we are forced to keep relatively many small teams anyway (x ≥ 3y and lower bounds). The bottleneck is the employee constraint; with small teams being more efficient, we should maximize employees used subject to constraints, and try not to let the less efficient large teams dominate.

Check the extreme with minimal large teams, \(y = 6\):

- From \(x \ge 3y\), \(x \ge 18\)  
- Use employees fully: \(3x + 5\cdot 6 \le 150 \Rightarrow 3x + 30 \le 150 \Rightarrow 3x \le 120 \Rightarrow x \le 40\)
- Best is \(x = 40\) (maximum possible, also ≥ 18 and ≥ 10):

Employees: \(3\cdot 40 + 5\cdot 6 = 120 + 30 = 150\)  
Lawn: \(50\cdot 40 + 80\cdot 6 = 2000 + 480 = 2480\)

Compare:
- \(x=33, y=10\): 2450 sq ft
- \(x=40, y=6\): 2480 sq ft  ← larger

We must also verify that increasing \(y\) above 6 while adjusting \(x\) doesn’t improve area. For any feasible solution using full employees \(3x+5y=150\), express \(x\) in terms of \(y\):

\[
x = \frac{150 - 5y}{3}
\]

Lawn area:
\[
Z(y) = 50x + 80y = 50\cdot \frac{150 - 5y}{3} + 80y
= \frac{7500 - 250y}{3} + 80y
= 2500 - \frac{250}{3}y + 80y
= 2500 + \frac{-250 + 240}{3}y
= 2500 - \frac{10}{3}y
\]

So for all full-employee solutions, Z strictly decreases as y increases. Thus the smallest feasible y (i.e., y = 6) with full employee usage gives the maximum area.

Conclusion:

- Optimal solution: \(x = 40\) small teams, \(y = 6\) large teams  
- Maximum lawn mowed: 2480 sq ft

---

### Formulation summary

Decision variables:

- \(x \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of small teams  
- \(y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of large teams  

Maximize:
\[
\max Z = 50x + 80y
\]

Subject to:
\[
\begin{aligned}
3x + 5y &\le 150 &\text{(employee availability)}\\
x &\ge 3y &\text{(small teams ≥ 3 × large teams)}\\
y &\ge 6 &\text{(minimum large teams)}\\
x &\ge 10 &\text{(minimum small teams)}\\
x, y &\in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

Optimal solution:
\[
x^* = 40,\quad y^* = 6,\quad Z^* = 2480 \text{ sq ft}
\]

### Python tools to solve it

This is a small integer linear programming (ILP) problem. Appropriate libraries:

- `PuLP` (lightweight, easy syntax)
- `ortools.linear_solver` from Google OR-Tools
- `mip` (Python-MIP)
- `cvxpy` (with an MILP-capable backend)

Example with PuLP:

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Lawn_Mowing_Teams", pl.LpMaximize)

# Variables
x = pl.LpVariable('small_teams', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
y = pl.LpVariable('large_teams', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective
prob += 50 * x + 80 * y

# Constraints
prob += 3 * x + 5 * y <= 150        # employees
prob += x >= 3 * y                  # small >= 3 * large
prob += y >= 6                      # min large teams
prob += x >= 10                     # min small teams

# Solve
prob.solve()

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Small teams:", x.value())
print("Large teams:", y.value())
print("Max lawn area:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

This will return the optimal solution \(x=40\), \(y=6\), \(Z=2480\).

[CODER]:
import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Define the problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Lawn_Mowing_Teams", pl.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables
    x = pl.LpVariable('small_teams', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    y = pl.LpVariable('large_teams', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective function: maximize lawn area
    prob += 50 * x + 80 * y

    # Constraints
    prob += 3 * x + 5 * y <= 150      # Employee availability
    prob += x >= 3 * y                # Small teams at least 3 times large teams
    prob += y >= 6                    # Minimum large teams
    prob += x >= 10                   # Minimum small teams

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get the optimal objective value
    final_obj = pl.value(prob.objective)

    # Print the final objective value in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
