Here is a full mathematical optimization model for the problem.

---

### 1. Decision Variables

Let  
- \(x_A \ge 0\): number of doses of medicine A to produce  
- \(x_B \ge 0\): number of doses of medicine B to produce  

Given the real-world context (“doses”), \(x_A\) and \(x_B\) should be **integers**. So:

\[
x_A, x_B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

### 2. Objective Function

Each dose of:
- Medicine A treats 12 people  
- Medicine B treats 8 people  

We want to **maximize the total number of people treated**:

\[
\max Z = 12x_A + 8x_B
\]

---

### 3. Constraints

#### a) Imported material constraint

Per dose:
- A uses 30 units
- B uses 40 units

Total available imported material: at most 300 units.

\[
30x_A + 40x_B \le 300
\]

#### b) mRNA constraint

Per dose:
- A uses 50 units
- B uses 30 units

Total available mRNA: at most 400 units.

\[
50x_A + 30x_B \le 400
\]

#### c) Production limit on medicine A

The lab can make at most 5 doses of medicine A:

\[
x_A \le 5
\]

#### d) Relationship between A and B

The number of doses of medicine B must be **larger than** the number of doses of medicine A:

\[
x_B \ge x_A + 1
\]

(“Larger than” → strict \(x_B > x_A\); in integer programming we can write it as \(x_B \ge x_A + 1\).)

#### e) Non-negativity and integrality

\[
x_A, x_B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

### 4. Complete Model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max\quad & Z = 12x_A + 8x_B \\
\text{s.t.}\quad 
& 30x_A + 40x_B \le 300 \\
& 50x_A + 30x_B \le 400 \\
& x_A \le 5 \\
& x_B \ge x_A + 1 \\
& x_A, x_B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 5. Suitable Python Libraries

This is a small **integer linear programming (ILP)** problem. All of the following libraries can solve it:

- **PuLP**: user-friendly, pure Python, good for teaching and small problems.
- **OR-Tools (Google)**: very fast, industrial-strength MILP solver.
- **Pyomo**: more general modeling framework, good for larger projects.
- **SciPy’s `scipy.optimize.linprog`**: only handles linear programming; you’d have to relax integrality or handle integer solutions manually, so less ideal here.

For a direct and simple implementation, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are the best choices.

import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Medicine_Production", pulp.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables: integer, non-negative
    x_A = pulp.LpVariable('x_A', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    x_B = pulp.LpVariable('x_B', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective function: maximize people treated
    prob += 12 * x_A + 8 * x_B

    # Constraints
    # Imported material constraint
    prob += 30 * x_A + 40 * x_B <= 300

    # mRNA constraint
    prob += 50 * x_A + 30 * x_B <= 400

    # Production limit on medicine A
    prob += x_A <= 5

    # Relationship between A and B: x_B >= x_A + 1
    prob += x_B >= x_A + 1

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print final objective in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
