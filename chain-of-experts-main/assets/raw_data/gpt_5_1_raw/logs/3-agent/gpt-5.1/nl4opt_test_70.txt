Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem as a linear program.

### 1. Decision variables

Let:

- \(x\) = number of trips made by the **new** company  
- \(y\) = number of trips made by the **old** company  

We will treat \(x\) and \(y\) as continuous nonnegative variables at first (LP). If you require trip counts to be integers, we then have an integer linear program (ILP).

### 2. Objective: Minimize diesel usage

Each trip by:
- new company uses 30 liters
- old company uses 40 liters

Total diesel used:

\[
\text{Minimize } Z = 30x + 40y
\]

### 3. Constraints

1. **Demand: at least 1000 gifts must be delivered**

   - New company: 50 gifts per trip  
   - Old company: 70 gifts per trip  

   So:
   \[
   50x + 70y \;\ge\; 1000
   \]

2. **New company trip limit: at most 15 trips**

   \[
   x \;\le\; 15
   \]

3. **Old company must handle at least 40% of all trips**

   At least 40% of all trips are by the old company:
   \[
   y \;\ge\; 0.4(x + y)
   \]

   Rearranging:
   \[
   y \;\ge\; 0.4x + 0.4y
   \]
   \[
   0.6y \;\ge\; 0.4x
   \]
   \[
   3y \;\ge\; 2x
   \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
   y \;\ge\; \tfrac{2}{3}x
   \]

4. **Nonnegativity**

   \[
   x \;\ge\; 0,\quad y \;\ge\; 0
   \]

5. **(Optional) Integrality**

   If we require whole trips:
   \[
   x, y \in \mathbb{Z}
   \]

---

### 4. Final mathematical model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & 30x + 40y \\
\text{s.t.}\quad 
& 50x + 70y \ge 1000 \\
& x \le 15 \\
& 3y \ge 2x \\
& x \ge 0,\; y \ge 0 \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \text{(if integer trips are enforced)}
\end{aligned}
\]

### 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small linear/integer linear optimization problem. Appropriate Python libraries:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`): Simple modeling of linear and integer programs in Python; comes with CBC solver by default.
- **OR-Tools Linear Solver** (`ortools.linear_solver`): Very efficient for LP/ILP, easy to define integer/binary variables.
- **Pyomo**: More general modeling framework, also supports LP/ILP.
- **SciPy** (`scipy.optimize.linprog`): Can solve the LP relaxation (without integrality). For integer constraints you’d need another library.

For this specific “how many trips” problem, I’d recommend **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** if you want integer solutions.

import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: minimize diesel usage
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Gift_Transport_Optimization", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: integer number of trips
    x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # trips by new company
    y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # trips by old company

    # Objective function: Minimize total diesel
    prob += 30 * x + 40 * y

    # Constraints
    # 1) Demand: at least 1000 gifts
    prob += 50 * x + 70 * y >= 1000, "Demand"

    # 2) New company trip limit
    prob += x <= 15, "New_Trip_Limit"

    # 3) Old company handles at least 40% of trips: 3y >= 2x
    prob += 3 * y >= 2 * x, "Old_Trip_Share"

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print final objective value in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
