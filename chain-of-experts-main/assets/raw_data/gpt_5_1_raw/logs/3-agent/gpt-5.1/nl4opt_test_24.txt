Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem and how to solve it.

---

## 1. Define decision variables

Let:

- \(L \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of large mobile production units  
- \(S \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of small mobile production units  

Both must be non‑negative integers.

---

## 2. Objective: minimize total parking spots

Each large unit uses 2 spots, each small unit uses 1 spot.  
Minimize total parking spots:

\[
\min \; Z = 2L + S
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### (a) Transport capacity (at least 80 people)

- Large unit capacity: 6 people  
- Small unit capacity: 2 people  

Total capacity must be at least 80:

\[
6L + 2S \;\ge\; 80
\]

---

### (b) Minimum number of small units

At least 5 small mobile units are required:

\[
S \;\ge\; 5
\]

---

### (c) Large units at least 75% of all vehicles

“Large mobile production units … must make up at least 75% of all vehicles”:

\[
L \;\ge\; 0.75(L + S)
\]

Rearrange:

\[
L \;\ge\; 0.75L + 0.75S \\
0.25L \;\ge\; 0.75S \\
L \;\ge\; 3S
\]

So:

\[
L \;\ge\; 3S
\]

---

### (d) Integrality and non-negativity

\[
L, S \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Complete optimization model (integer linear program)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & Z = 2L + S \\
\text{s.t.} \quad 
& 6L + 2S \;\ge\; 80 \\
& S \;\ge\; 5 \\
& L \;\ge\; 3S \\
& L, S \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small integer linear program. Appropriate libraries:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`): easy modeling of linear and integer programs; good for teaching and small to medium problems.
- **OR-Tools** (`ortools.linear_solver` or `ortools.sat.python.cp_model`): powerful Google library, good performance and multiple solvers.
- **Pyomo**: more general modeling environment for optimization, but a bit heavier-weight than PuLP for this size.
- **SciPy** (`scipy.optimize.linprog`) is primarily for continuous LP; not ideal here because we need integrality.

For this problem specifically, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are the most straightforward choices.

---

## 6. Example with PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Create problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Production_Units", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables (non-negative integers)
L = pl.LpVariable('L', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
S = pl.LpVariable('S', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective: minimize total parking spots
prob += 2*L + S

# Constraints
prob += 6*L + 2*S >= 80      # capacity
prob += S >= 5               # minimum small units
prob += L >= 3*S             # large units at least 75% of total

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Large units (L):", L.value())
print("Small units (S):", S.value())
print("Total parking spots:", 2*L.value() + S.value())
```

You can swap PuLP with OR-Tools if you prefer:

- `ortools.linear_solver.pywraplp.Solver` for an LP/MIP-style formulation, or
- `ortools.sat.python.cp_model` for a CP-SAT integer model.
