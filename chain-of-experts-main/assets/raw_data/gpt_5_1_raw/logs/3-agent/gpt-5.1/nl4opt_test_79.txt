Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem and how to solve it as an optimization problem.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x_1 \ge 0\): number of doses of pain killer 1  
- \(x_2 \ge 0\): number of doses of pain killer 2  

We assume fractional doses are allowed unless the medical context explicitly requires integers.

---

## 2. Constraints

### a) Sleep medicine constraint (at most 8 units)

- Pain killer 1 gives 0.3 units of sleep medicine per dose.  
- Pain killer 2 gives 0.6 units of sleep medicine per dose.  

Total sleep medicine must not exceed 8 units:

\[
0.3 x_1 + 0.6 x_2 \le 8
\]

### b) Legs medicine constraint (at least 4 units)

- Pain killer 1 gives 0.5 units to the legs per dose.  
- Pain killer 2 gives 0.7 units to the legs per dose.  

Total medicine to the legs must be at least 4 units:

\[
0.5 x_1 + 0.7 x_2 \ge 4
\]

### c) Nonnegativity

\[
x_1 \ge 0,\quad x_2 \ge 0
\]

---

## 3. Objective function

We want to maximize medicine delivered to the back.

- Pain killer 1 gives 0.8 units to the back per dose.  
- Pain killer 2 gives 0.4 units to the back per dose.  

So the objective is:

\[
\text{Maximize } Z = 0.8 x_1 + 0.4 x_2
\]

---

## 4. Complete linear program

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max_{x_1, x_2} \quad & 0.8 x_1 + 0.4 x_2 \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 0.3 x_1 + 0.6 x_2 \le 8 \quad &&\text{(sleep medicine)} \\
& 0.5 x_1 + 0.7 x_2 \ge 4 \quad &&\text{(legs medicine)} \\
& x_1 \ge 0,\; x_2 \ge 0 \quad &&\text{(nonnegativity)}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is a standard linear programming (LP) problem.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

You can solve this LP with any of the following:

- `PuLP` (very user-friendly for LP/MIP, pure Python)
- `scipy.optimize.linprog` (in SciPy; good for continuous LP)
- `ortools.linear_solver` (Google OR-Tools; powerful and fast)

For this problem, `PuLP` or `scipy.optimize.linprog` are ideal.

---

## 6. Example solution in Python (using PuLP)

```python
import pulp as pl

# Define problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Painkiller_Back_Maximization", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables (nonnegative)
x1 = pl.LpVariable('x1', lowBound=0)  # doses of pain killer 1
x2 = pl.LpVariable('x2', lowBound=0)  # doses of pain killer 2

# Objective: maximize medicine to the back
prob += 0.8 * x1 + 0.4 * x2, "Back_Medicine"

# Constraints
prob += 0.3 * x1 + 0.6 * x2 <= 8, "Sleep_Upper"
prob += 0.5 * x1 + 0.7 * x2 >= 4, "Legs_Lower"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

# Results
print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Optimal x1 (doses of pain killer 1):", x1.value())
print("Optimal x2 (doses of pain killer 2):", x2.value())
print("Max back medicine:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```
