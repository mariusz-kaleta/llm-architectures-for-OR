[MODELER]:
Here is a clean optimization model of the problem and how to solve it.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x\) = number of bus trips  
- \(y\) = number of car trips  

We’ll treat \(x, y\) as **nonnegative integers** (you can’t do a fraction of a trip).

---

## 2. Objective

We want to minimize the **total transport time**.

- Each bus trip: 2 hours → total bus time = \(2x\)  
- Each car trip: 1.5 hours → total car time = \(1.5y\)

**Objective function**:
\[
\min Z = 2x + 1.5y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1 Capacity (transport all 1200 chickens)

- Each bus trip carries 100 chickens → total bus capacity = \(100x\)
- Each car trip carries 40 chickens → total car capacity = \(40y\)

All 1200 chickens must be transported:
\[
100x + 40y \;\ge\; 1200
\]

(If you interpret “needs to transport 1200 chicken” as exactly, you could write \(= 1200\), but usually “at least” is fine and optimal solutions tend not to overshoot too much because time is being minimized.)

---

### 3.2 Limit on bus trips

At most 10 bus trips:
\[
x \;\le\; 10
\]

---

### 3.3 At least 60% of trips by car

Total trips = \(x + y\). At least 60% of those must be by car:
\[
\frac{y}{x + y} \;\ge\; 0.6
\]

Rearrange:
\[
y \;\ge\; 0.6(x + y)  
\Rightarrow y \;\ge\; 0.6x + 0.6y  
\Rightarrow y - 0.6y \;\ge\; 0.6x  
\Rightarrow 0.4y \;\ge\; 0.6x  
\Rightarrow y \;\ge\; 1.5x
\]

So:
\[
y \;\ge\; 1.5x
\]

Since \(x, y\) are integers, this effectively means \(y \ge \lceil 1.5x \rceil\).

---

### 3.4 Non-negativity and integrality

\[
x \ge 0,\;\; y \ge 0,\;\; x,y \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

---

## 4. Full model

\[
\begin{align}
\min\;\; & Z = 2x + 1.5y \\
\text{s.t.}\;\; 
& 100x + 40y \ge 1200 \\
& x \le 10 \\
& y \ge 1.5x \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{align}
\]

---

## 5. Solving manually / structure

If you want a quick reasoning:

- From \(y \ge 1.5x\), cars grow at least proportional to buses.
- To satisfy capacity: \(100x + 40y \ge 1200\).
- Because car trips are shorter (1.5 h vs 2 h), and there’s a minimum ratio \(y \ge 1.5x\), the model will likely push toward using as many car trips and fewer bus trips as capacity and constraints allow.

This is a **small integer linear programming (ILP)** problem.

---

## 6. Suitable Python libraries

You can model and solve this with any MILP-capable library, for example:

- **PuLP** (pure Python, easy to use, works with CBC by default)
- **OR-Tools** (Google’s optimization suite, powerful MILP solvers)
- **Pyomo** (general algebraic modeling, can link to CBC, GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc.)
- **SciPy** is not ideal here because we need integer variables.

### Example with PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("ChickenTransport", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables (integer)
x = pl.LpVariable('bus_trips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
y = pl.LpVariable('car_trips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective: minimize total time
prob += 2 * x + 1.5 * y

# Constraints
prob += 100 * x + 40 * y >= 1200        # capacity
prob += x <= 10                         # max bus trips
prob += y >= 1.5 * x                    # at least 60% trips by car

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Bus trips:", pl.value(x))
print("Car trips:", pl.value(y))
print("Total time:", pl.value(2 * x + 1.5 * y))
```

You can adapt the same model almost identically in OR-Tools or Pyomo.

[CODER]:
import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Define the problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("ChickenTransport", pl.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: integer, non-negative
    x = pl.LpVariable('bus_trips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    y = pl.LpVariable('car_trips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective: minimize total time
    prob += 2 * x + 1.5 * y

    # Constraints
    prob += 100 * x + 40 * y >= 1200        # capacity
    prob += x <= 10                         # max bus trips
    prob += y >= 1.5 * x                    # at least 60% trips by car

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Compute final objective value
    final_obj = pl.value(2 * x + 1.5 * y)

    # Print final objective in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
