Here’s a linear programming model for this mixing problem.

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x \ge 0\): kilograms of **regular mix** produced  
- \(y \ge 0\): kilograms of **sour surprise mix** produced  

## 2. Objective

Maximize total profit:

\[
\max\; Z = 3x + 5y
\]

where  
- 3 is the profit per kg of regular mix  
- 5 is the profit per kg of sour surprise mix  

## 3. Constraints

### a) Regular candy availability

Each kg of:
- regular mix uses 0.8 kg of regular candy  
- sour surprise mix uses 0.1 kg of regular candy  

Total regular candy available is 80 kg:

\[
0.8x + 0.1y \le 80
\]

### b) Sour candy availability

Each kg of:
- regular mix uses 0.2 kg of sour candy  
- sour surprise mix uses 0.9 kg of sour candy  

Total sour candy available is 60 kg:

\[
0.2x + 0.9y \le 60
\]

### c) Non-negativity

\[
x \ge 0,\quad y \ge 0
\]

So the full LP is:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max\quad & 3x + 5y \\
\text{s.t.}\quad
& 0.8x + 0.1y \le 80 \\
& 0.2x + 0.9y \le 60 \\
& x \ge 0,\; y \ge 0
\end{aligned}
\]

## 4. Solving the model (outline)

Suitable Python libraries:
- `PuLP` – simple modeling of LP problems; integrates CBC solver.
- `scipy.optimize.linprog` – basic LP solver.
- `ortools.linear_solver` – Google OR-Tools, very capable for LP/MIP.

### Example with PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("CandyMix", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables
x = pl.LpVariable('regular_mix', lowBound=0)
y = pl.LpVariable('sour_mix', lowBound=0)

# Objective
prob += 3 * x + 5 * y

# Constraints
prob += 0.8 * x + 0.1 * y <= 80     # regular candy
prob += 0.2 * x + 0.9 * y <= 60     # sour candy

# Solve
prob.solve()

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Regular mix (kg):", x.value())
print("Sour surprise mix (kg):", y.value())
print("Max profit:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

This will compute the optimal kilograms of each mix to maximize profits under the candy availability constraints.
