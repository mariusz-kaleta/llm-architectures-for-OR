[PLANNER]:
1. **Key optimisation goal**

- **Goal:** Minimise the number of taxi rides needed, while:
  - Transporting at least 500 employees.
  - Respecting the limit on the share of company car rides.
  - Respecting the minimum number of company car rides.

---

2. **Suggested model type**

- **Model:** Integer Linear Programming (ILP), with:
  - Two main integer decision variables (number of rides of each type).
  - Linear constraints for capacity and policy limits.

---

3. **Main decision variables**

Define two non‑negative integer variables:

- \(T\): Number of taxi rides.
- \(C\): Number of company car rides.

(If helpful, you can also define an auxiliary variable for total rides \(R = T + C\), but it can be kept implicit via expressions in the constraints.)

---

4. **Key constraints and how to represent them**

You should structure the model around the following constraint groups:

1. **Capacity requirement (employee transport):**
   - Each taxi ride carries 2 employees.
   - Each company car ride carries 3 employees.
   - Total employees transported must be **at least 500**.
   - Constraint form:  
     - Total carried by taxis + total carried by cars ≥ 500 employees.

2. **Share (percentage) constraint on company car rides:**
   - “At most 60% of the rides can be company car rides”:
     - The proportion of rides that are company car rides is at most 0.6.
     - Use the ratio: number of car rides / total rides ≤ 0.6.
     - This involves \(C / (T + C) ≤ 0.6\).  
     - Convert this to a linear inequality by cross-multiplying to eliminate the fraction (assuming total rides > 0): an inequality linking \(C\) and \(T\).
   - Be careful to ensure the direction of the inequality is correct.

3. **Minimum number of company car rides:**
   - “At least 30 company car rides”:
     - Direct lower bound on \(C\).

4. **Non-negativity and integrality:**
   - Both \(T\) and \(C\) must be:
     - Non‑negative.
     - Integer (because you can’t have fractional rides).

---

5. **Objective function structure**

- Objective: **Minimise \(T\)** (the number of taxi rides), subject to all constraints above.
  - No direct cost is associated with cars in the objective; the car usage is implicitly limited by the share and minimum constraints.
  - This is a single-objective ILP with a simple linear objective.

---

6. **Modelling notes / checks for the Modeller**

- Ensure the ratio constraint (company car rides ≤ 60% of all rides) is written in a linear form; that’s the main modelling subtlety.
- After modelling:
  - Check feasibility: make sure there exists some combination of \(T, C\) satisfying:
    - Capacity ≥ 500.
    - \(C\) ≥ 30.
    - Company cars ≤ 60% of all rides.
  - Once solved, verify:
    - The solution uses an integer number of rides.
    - The total number of employees transported meets or exceeds 500.
    - The company car rides proportion is ≤ 60% and C ≥ 30.

This structure is sufficient for the Modeller to implement and solve the optimisation problem.

[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical formulation of the problem as an Integer Linear Program (ILP), followed by suggestions for Python libraries.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(T \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of taxi rides  
- \(C \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of company car rides  

(Optionally, total rides \(R = T + C\) can be used as a derived quantity.)

---

## 2. Objective

Minimise the number of taxi rides:

\[
\min T
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1 Capacity (employees transported)

Each taxi ride carries 2 employees; each company car ride carries 3 employees. At least 500 employees must be transported:

\[
2T + 3C \;\ge\; 500
\]

---

### 3.2 Share constraint on company car rides (≤ 60%)

At most 60% of *all rides* can be company car rides:

\[
\frac{C}{T + C} \;\le\; 0.6
\]

Assume \(T + C > 0\) (trivially true because at least 500 employees must be moved). Cross‑multiply to linearise:

\[
C \;\le\; 0.6(T + C)
\]

\[
C \;\le\; 0.6T + 0.6C
\]

\[
C - 0.6C \;\le\; 0.6T
\]

\[
0.4C \;\le\; 0.6T
\]

\[
2C \;\le\; 3T
\]

So the linear form of the share constraint is:

\[
2C \;\le\; 3T
\]

---

### 3.3 Minimum number of company car rides

At least 30 company car rides:

\[
C \;\ge\; 30
\]

---

### 3.4 Non‑negativity and integrality

\[
T \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0},\quad C \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Complete model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & T \\
\text{s.t.} \quad 
& 2T + 3C \;\ge\; 500 & \text{(capacity)} \\
& 2C \;\le\; 3T       & \text{(≤ 60% cars)} \\
& C \;\ge\; 30        & \text{(min cars)} \\
& T, C \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} & \text{(integrality)}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is a small, standard ILP.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

For this kind of integer linear problem, good choices are:

- **PuLP**  
  - Simple modelling interface, uses open‑source solvers (CBC by default).  
  - Good for teaching, small to medium problems.
- **OR‑Tools (Google OR‑Tools, `ortools.linear_solver`)**  
  - Industrial‑strength, very fast; supports CBC, GLOP, SCIP, etc.  
  - Clean Python API for linear and integer programming.
- **Pyomo**  
  - More general algebraic modelling language; integrates with many solvers (CBC, GLPK, CPLEX, Gurobi, etc.).  
  - Good if you plan to extend to more complex models.
- **SciPy (`scipy.optimize`)**  
  - Mainly for continuous optimisation; not ideal for pure integer problems like this unless you relax integrality, so it’s not the preferred tool here.

For a problem exactly like this (small ILP, educational/operational use), **PuLP** or **OR‑Tools** are the most straightforward and practical choices.

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: minimize number of taxi rides T
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Taxi_CompanyCar_Problem", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables
    T = pulp.LpVariable('T', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # Taxi rides
    C = pulp.LpVariable('C', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # Company car rides

    # Objective: minimize T
    prob += T

    # Constraints

    # Capacity: 2T + 3C >= 500
    prob += 2 * T + 3 * C >= 500, "Capacity"

    # Share constraint: 2C <= 3T
    prob += 2 * C <= 3 * T, "ShareConstraint"

    # Minimum company car rides: C >= 30
    prob += C >= 30, "MinCompanyCars"

    # Solve the problem using default solver (CBC)
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract values
    T_val = pulp.value(T)
    C_val = pulp.value(C)

    # Objective value is T (number of taxi rides)
    final_obj = T_val

    # Optionally, ensure integrality in case of solver tolerances
    if final_obj is not None:
        final_obj = int(round(final_obj))

    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
