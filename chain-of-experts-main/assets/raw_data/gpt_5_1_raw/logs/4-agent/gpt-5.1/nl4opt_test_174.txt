[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
Goal: Maximise the total number of patients who can have their temperature taken during the 15,000 minutes the office is open.

2. Suggested model type  
Model: Linear Programming (LP) with integer (or non‑negative whole number) decision variables, since the number of patients must be integral.

3. Main decision variables  
Define two primary decision variables:
- \(E\): Number of patients whose temperature is taken with the electronic thermometer.  
- \(R\): Number of patients whose temperature is taken with the regular thermometer.

(If the modeller wishes, they can explicitly declare them as integer, but the structure is simple enough that an LP relaxation will naturally yield integers.)

4. Key constraints to consider  

a) Time capacity constraint  
- Each electronic reading takes 3 minutes; each regular reading takes 2 minutes.  
- Total available time is 15,000 minutes.  
- Constraint: Total time for all electronic and regular measurements cannot exceed 15,000 minutes.  

b) Accuracy preference constraint  
- At least twice as many patients must be checked with the electronic thermometer as with the regular thermometer, to reflect higher accuracy.  
- Constraint: Number of electronic measurements is at least 2 times the number of regular measurements.  

c) Minimum usage of regular thermometer  
- Due to cooldown/operational requirements, at least 50 patients must be measured with the regular thermometer.  
- Constraint: Number of regular thermometer measurements is at least 50.  

d) Non-negativity and integrality  
- Both decision variables must be non-negative.  
- Both should be whole numbers (patients cannot be fractional).  

5. Objective function (verbally)  
- Maximise the total number of patients measured, i.e., maximise \(E + R\) subject to the above constraints.  

6. Modelling strategy notes for the Modeller  
- Start by formulating the LP with the variables and the three key constraints (time, ratio, minimum R), plus non-negativity.  
- Because the coefficients and right-hand sides are small integers, solving the LP directly should produce integer solutions; formal integer programming might not be necessary.  
- After solving, verify feasibility against:  
  - Total time used.  
  - The ratio condition (electronic at least twice regular).  
  - The minimum count of regular thermometer measurements.  
- Finally, confirm that the solution indeed maximises the total number of patients (no unused time that could be converted into additional measurements without violating constraints).

[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical model and how you could implement/solve it in Python.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(E \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of patients checked with the electronic thermometer  
- \(R \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of patients checked with the regular thermometer  

(You can model them as continuous \(\ge 0\) and the LP solution will still be integral because this is a simple problem with integral coefficients.)

---

## 2. Objective

Maximise the total number of patients whose temperature is taken:

\[
\max \; Z = E + R
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

1. **Time capacity constraint**

   - Electronic reading: 3 minutes per patient  
   - Regular reading: 2 minutes per patient  
   - Office open: 15,000 minutes  

   Total time used cannot exceed available time:

   \[
   3E + 2R \le 15000
   \]

2. **Accuracy preference (electronic at least twice regular)**

   \[
   E \ge 2R
   \]

3. **Minimum usage of regular thermometer**

   \[
   R \ge 50
   \]

4. **Non-negativity and integrality**

   \[
   E \ge 0,\quad R \ge 0,\quad E, R \in \mathbb{Z}
   \]

---

## 4. Full model summary

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max \quad & E + R \\
\text{s.t.} \quad & 3E + 2R \le 15000 \\
                  & E - 2R \ge 0 \\
                  & R \ge 50 \\
                  & E, R \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a very small linear/integer program. Any standard LP/MIP library will solve it instantly:

- **PuLP** (pure Python, easy to use, works with CBC by default)
- **OR-Tools** (Google OR-Tools, very fast MIP solver)
- **SciPy (`scipy.optimize.linprog`)** (solve as continuous LP; integrality is implicit here)
- **Pyomo** (if you want a more general algebraic modelling language)

For a direct and simple implementation, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are recommended.

---

## 6. Example with PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Create problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Temperature_Maximisation", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables
E = pl.LpVariable("E", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")
R = pl.LpVariable("R", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")

# Objective
prob += E + R, "Total_Patients"

# Constraints
prob += 3*E + 2*R <= 15000, "Time_Capacity"
prob += E >= 2*R, "Accuracy_Preference"
prob += R >= 50, "Min_Regular_Usage"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("E (electronic):", pl.value(E))
print("R (regular):", pl.value(R))
print("Total patients:", pl.value(E + R))
print("Time used:", 3*pl.value(E) + 2*pl.value(R))
```

You can substitute PuLP with OR-Tools or Pyomo if you prefer different modelling styles; the underlying mathematical model remains exactly as above.

[CODER]:
import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Create problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Temperature_Maximisation", pl.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables
    E = pl.LpVariable("E", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")
    R = pl.LpVariable("R", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")

    # Objective: Maximise total patients
    prob += E + R, "Total_Patients"

    # Constraints
    prob += 3 * E + 2 * R <= 15000, "Time_Capacity"
    prob += E >= 2 * R, "Accuracy_Preference"
    prob += R >= 50, "Min_Regular_Usage"

    # Solve
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract objective value
    obj_value = pl.value(E + R)

    # Final required print
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {obj_value}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
