[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
Goal: Minimise the total cost of the feed mixture while meeting minimum protein and fat requirements.

2. Suggested model type  
Model: Linear Programming (LP), two-variable continuous optimization.

3. Main decision variables  
Define continuous non‑negative variables such as:
- \(x_A\): kilograms of Feed A to include in the mixture  
- \(x_B\): kilograms of Feed B to include in the mixture  

(Units are kilograms to match the cost data and nutrient content per kg.)

4. Key constraints to consider  

Nutrient requirements:
- Protein requirement:  
  The mixture must contain at least 30 units of protein.  
  Each kg of Feed A contributes 10 protein units; each kg of Feed B contributes 7 protein units.  
  → Constraint: total protein from \(x_A\) and \(x_B\) ≥ 30.

- Fat requirement:  
  The mixture must contain at least 50 units of fat.  
  Each kg of Feed A contributes 8 fat units; each kg of Feed B contributes 15 fat units.  
  → Constraint: total fat from \(x_A\) and \(x_B\) ≥ 50.

Non‑negativity:
- Cannot use negative quantities of feed:  
  → \(x_A \ge 0\), \(x_B \ge 0\).

Objective function components:
- Cost:  
  Feed A costs $100 per kg; Feed B costs $80 per kg.  
  → Total cost = 100·\(x_A\) + 80·\(x_B\).  
  The LP should minimise this expression subject to the above constraints.

5. Modelling strategy notes  

- The problem is small (two variables, two binding nutrient constraints plus non‑negativity). It can be solved graphically or using any LP solver.  
- Check feasibility: intersection of nutrient constraints with non‑negativity region should exist because each feed contributes positively to both nutrients.  
- Expect the optimal solution at an intersection (corner point) of the protein and fat constraints, unless one constraint is redundant.  
- After obtaining \(x_A, x_B\), compute the minimum cost by plugging into the cost expression, and verify that protein and fat requirements are both satisfied (≥ 30 protein, ≥ 50 fat).

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_A \ge 0\): kilograms of Feed A in the mixture  
- \(x_B \ge 0\): kilograms of Feed B in the mixture  

These are continuous (can take any non‑negative real values).

---

## 2. Objective

Minimise the total cost of the feed mixture:

\[
\text{Minimise } Z = 100 x_A + 80 x_B
\]

where:
- 100 = cost ($) per kg of Feed A  
- 80 = cost ($) per kg of Feed B  

---

## 3. Constraints

### Protein requirement

The mixture must supply at least 30 units of protein.

- Feed A: 10 units protein per kg  
- Feed B: 7 units protein per kg  

\[
10 x_A + 7 x_B \;\ge\; 30
\]

### Fat requirement

The mixture must supply at least 50 units of fat.

- Feed A: 8 units fat per kg  
- Feed B: 15 units fat per kg  

\[
8 x_A + 15 x_B \;\ge\; 50
\]

### Non‑negativity

\[
x_A \ge 0,\quad x_B \ge 0
\]

---

## 4. Full LP formulation

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Minimise} \quad & Z = 100 x_A + 80 x_B \\
\text{subject to} \quad
& 10 x_A + 7 x_B \ge 30 \quad \text{(protein)} \\
& 8 x_A + 15 x_B \ge 50 \quad \text{(fat)} \\
& x_A \ge 0,\; x_B \ge 0
\end{aligned}
\]

This is a standard linear program with 2 variables and 2 main constraints.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any LP solver is suitable; recommended options:

- `PuLP` (easy modeling interface, pure Python, uses CBC by default)
- `scipy.optimize.linprog` (good for small LPs, part of SciPy)
- `ortools.linear_solver` from Google OR-Tools (industrial-grade)

For this example, `PuLP` is especially straightforward.

---

## 6. Example implementation in Python (PuLP)

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Feed_Mix_Cost_Minimization", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables
x_A = pl.LpVariable('x_A', lowBound=0)  # kg of Feed A
x_B = pl.LpVariable('x_B', lowBound=0)  # kg of Feed B

# Objective function: Minimize cost
prob += 100 * x_A + 80 * x_B, "Total_Cost"

# Constraints
prob += 10 * x_A + 7 * x_B >= 30, "Protein_requirement"
prob += 8 * x_A + 15 * x_B >= 50, "Fat_requirement"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

# Results
status = pl.LpStatus[prob.status]
x_A_opt = x_A.varValue
x_B_opt = x_B.varValue
min_cost = pl.value(prob.objective)

print("Status:", status)
print("Optimal x_A (kg):", x_A_opt)
print("Optimal x_B (kg):", x_B_opt)
print("Minimum cost ($):", min_cost)
```

You can swap PuLP with `scipy.optimize.linprog` or OR-Tools if you prefer, but the model (variables, objective, constraints) remains exactly the same.
