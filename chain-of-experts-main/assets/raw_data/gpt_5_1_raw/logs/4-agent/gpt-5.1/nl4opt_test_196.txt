[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
Goal: Maximise total advertising exposure (total expected number of viewers reached) subject to the budget and policy limits on numbers of ads.

2. Suggested model type  
Model: Integer Linear Programming (ILP) – variables are counts of ads, so they must be non‑negative integers.

3. Main decision variables  
Define two primary decision variables:

- Number of radio ads to purchase.  
- Number of social media ads to purchase.

Both must be integer and non‑negative.

4. Objective function (conceptual)  
Maximise:  
- Total exposure = (exposure per radio ad) × (number of radio ads)  
  + (exposure per social media ad) × (number of social media ads).

Numerical components for the modeller to use:  
- Radio ad exposure = 60,500 viewers/ad.  
- Social media ad exposure = 50,000 viewers/ad.

5. Key constraints to consider  

a) Budget constraint  
- Total spending on radio ads plus total spending on social media ads cannot exceed $250,000.  
- Radio ad cost = $5,000 per ad.  
- Social media ad cost = $9,150 per ad.  
- Formulate:  
  (cost per radio ad × number of radio ads)  
  + (cost per social media ad × number of social media ads)  
  ≤ total budget ($250,000).

b) Radio ads quantity constraints  
- Minimum number of radio ads: at least 15.  
- Maximum number of radio ads: no more than 40.  
- Represent as lower and upper bounds on the radio-ad variable.

c) Social media ads quantity constraints  
- Minimum number of social media ads: at least 35.  
- No explicit upper bound beyond what the budget implies, but you can treat the budget as implicitly constraining the maximum feasible number of social media ads.  
- Represent as a lower bound on the social-media-ad variable.

d) Integrality constraints  
- Both decision variables must be integers (you can’t buy fractional ads).

6. Modelling strategy notes  

- This is a small two-variable ILP; it can be solved via:  
  - Any standard ILP solver, or  
  - Enumeration of feasible integer points satisfying all constraints and selecting the one with maximum exposure (for pedagogical or manual solution).  
- You may optionally first solve the LP relaxation (ignoring integrality) to identify the region and then search around the optimal fractional solution for nearby integer points.  
- Check corner points where the budget is binding and bounds on radio ads (min or max) are binding, since these are likely candidates for the optimum in such a simple linear model.

[MODELER]:
Here is a clear mathematical model of the problem.

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of radio ads to purchase  
- \(y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of social media ads to purchase  

Both are non‑negative integers.

## 2. Objective function

Maximise total expected exposure (number of viewers reached):

\[
\text{Maximise } Z = 60500\,x + 50000\,y
\]

where  
- 60,500 = expected viewers per radio ad  
- 50,000 = expected viewers per social media ad  

## 3. Constraints

### a) Budget constraint

Total spending cannot exceed \$250,000:

\[
5000\,x + 9150\,y \;\le\; 250000
\]

where  
- \$5,000 = cost per radio ad  
- \$9,150 = cost per social media ad  

### b) Radio ads quantity constraints

At least 15 and no more than 40 radio ads:

\[
15 \;\le\; x \;\le\; 40
\]

### c) Social media ads quantity constraint

At least 35 social media ads:

\[
y \;\ge\; 35
\]

(There is no explicit upper bound on \(y\); the budget and non‑negativity implicitly bound it.)

### d) Integrality and non‑negativity

\[
x, y \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad x \ge 0, \; y \ge 0
\]

Putting it together, this is a small Integer Linear Program (ILP).

---

## 4. Compact ILP formulation

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Maximise} \quad & Z = 60500x + 50000y \\
\text{subject to} \quad
& 5000x + 9150y \le 250000 \\
& 15 \le x \le 40 \\
& y \ge 35 \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any standard MILP/ILP solver works. For this problem, all of these are suitable and very easy to use:

- `PuLP` (pure Python, can use CBC solver; good for teaching and small models)
- `ortools.linear_solver` (Google OR-Tools; efficient and well-documented)
- `Pyomo` (more general modelling framework; good if integrating into larger optimisation workflows)
- `scipy.optimize` is **not** ideal here because it does not handle integer decision variables natively; you’d need a custom enumeration for integrality.

For a straightforward educational implementation, I recommend:

- **First choice:** `PuLP`
- **Alternative:** OR-Tools’ `pywraplp.Solver` with `CBC_MIXED_INTEGER_PROGRAMMING` or `SCIP_MIXED_INTEGER_PROGRAMMING`.

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Ad_Selection_Problem", pulp.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables (non-negative integers)
    x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # radio ads
    y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # social media ads

    # Objective function: Maximise exposure
    prob += 60500 * x + 50000 * y

    # Constraints
    prob += 5000 * x + 9150 * y <= 250000          # Budget
    prob += x >= 15                                # Min radio ads
    prob += x <= 40                                # Max radio ads
    prob += y >= 35                                # Min social media ads

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get objective value
    objective_value = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {objective_value}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
