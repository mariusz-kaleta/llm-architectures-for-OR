1. Key optimisation goal  
   - Goal: Minimise total diesel consumption used by both shipping companies, subject to meeting delivery and policy requirements.

2. Suggested model type  
   - Model: Integer Linear Programming (ILP)  
   Rationale:  
   - Decision variables (number of trips) must be integers.  
   - Objective and constraints are all linear in those variables.

3. Main decision variables  
   Define integer, non‑negative variables:  
   - NewCompanyTrips = number of trips made by the new company  
   - OldCompanyTrips = number of trips made by the old company  

4. Key constraints to consider  

   a) Delivery requirement (capacity constraint)  
   - Gifts delivered by new company: 50 per trip  
   - Gifts delivered by old company: 70 per trip  
   - Total gifts delivered must be at least 1000.  
   - Constraint: 50·NewCompanyTrips + 70·OldCompanyTrips ≥ 1000

   b) Trip limit for new company  
   - New company can make at most 15 trips.  
   - Constraint: NewCompanyTrips ≤ 15

   c) Minimum share of trips for the old company (40% rule)  
   - At least 40% of all trips must be made by the old company.  
   - Let TotalTrips = NewCompanyTrips + OldCompanyTrips.  
   - Requirement: OldCompanyTrips ≥ 0.4·TotalTrips  
   - This becomes a linear constraint in the two trip variables.  
   - Ensure the modeller correctly rearranges this into standard linear form.

   d) Non-negativity and integrality  
   - NewCompanyTrips ≥ 0, OldCompanyTrips ≥ 0  
   - Both must be integer-valued.

5. Objective function components  

   a) Diesel use per trip  
   - New company uses 30 liters per trip.  
   - Old company uses 40 liters per trip.

   b) Total diesel to minimise  
   - TotalDiesel = 30·NewCompanyTrips + 40·OldCompanyTrips  
   - Objective: Minimise TotalDiesel.

6. Modelling strategy notes for the Modeller  

   - Treat this as a small ILP with only two integer variables; exact solution via enumeration or any standard ILP solver is straightforward.  
   - The 40% requirement is the only slightly non-obvious constraint—express it directly in terms of the two variables and simplify to maintain linearity.  
   - After solving, verify that:  
     - The gift requirement is satisfied (≥ 1000).  
     - The new company trip upper bound is not violated.  
     - The old company’s share of trips is at least 40%.  
   - Because the variable set is tiny, the modeller may also check the optimal solution manually as a sanity check.

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_N \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of trips made by the new company  
- \(x_O \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of trips made by the old company  

Both must be non‑negative integers.

---

## 2. Objective

Minimise the total diesel used:

\[
\text{Minimise } Z = 30\,x_N + 40\,x_O
\]

where:
- 30 = liters of diesel per trip (new company)
- 40 = liters of diesel per trip (old company)

---

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1 Delivery requirement (capacity)

Each trip delivers:

- New company: 50 gifts per trip  
- Old company: 70 gifts per trip  

At least 1000 gifts must be delivered:

\[
50\,x_N + 70\,x_O \;\ge\; 1000
\]

---

### 3.2 Trip limit for the new company

At most 15 trips can be made by the new company:

\[
x_N \;\le\; 15
\]

---

### 3.3 Minimum share of trips for the old company (40% rule)

Let total trips be:

\[
T = x_N + x_O
\]

At least 40% of trips must be made by the old company:

\[
x_O \;\ge\; 0.4 \, (x_N + x_O)
\]

Rearrange to a standard linear form:

\[
x_O \;\ge\; 0.4x_N + 0.4x_O
\]

\[
x_O - 0.4x_O \;\ge\; 0.4x_N
\]

\[
0.6x_O \;\ge\; 0.4x_N
\]

\[
3x_O \;\ge\; 2x_N
\]

So the linear constraint to use is:

\[
3x_O - 2x_N \;\ge\; 0
\]

---

### 3.4 Non‑negativity and integrality

\[
x_N \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}, \qquad x_O \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Formal model summary

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min_{x_N, x_O} \quad & 30x_N + 40x_O \\
\text{s.t.} \quad 
& 50x_N + 70x_O \ge 1000 \quad &\text{(delivery requirement)} \\
& x_N \le 15 \quad &\text{(new company trip limit)} \\
& 3x_O - 2x_N \ge 0 \quad &\text{(old company \(\ge 40\%\) of trips)} \\
& x_N, x_O \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} \quad &\text{(non-negativity, integrality)}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is an Integer Linear Programming (ILP) problem with 2 integer decision variables and linear objective/constraints.

## 6. Example in Python (using PuLP)

```python
import pulp as pl

# Create problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Gift_Delivery", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables (non-negative integers)
x_N = pl.LpVariable('NewCompanyTrips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
x_O = pl.LpVariable('OldCompanyTrips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective: minimize total diesel
prob += 30 * x_N + 40 * x_O, "Total_Diesel"

# Constraints
prob += 50 * x_N + 70 * x_O >= 1000, "DeliveryRequirement"
prob += x_N <= 15, "NewCompanyTripLimit"
prob += 3 * x_O - 2 * x_N >= 0, "OldCompanyShare"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("New company trips:", pl.value(x_N))
print("Old company trips:", pl.value(x_O))
print("Minimum diesel used:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

You can use an analogous formulation in OR-Tools or Pyomo by directly translating the objective and constraints above.

import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Create problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Gift_Delivery", pl.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables (non-negative integers)
    x_N = pl.LpVariable('NewCompanyTrips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    x_O = pl.LpVariable('OldCompanyTrips', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective: minimize total diesel
    prob += 30 * x_N + 40 * x_O, "Total_Diesel"

    # Constraints
    prob += 50 * x_N + 70 * x_O >= 1000, "DeliveryRequirement"
    prob += x_N <= 15, "NewCompanyTripLimit"
    prob += 3 * x_O - 2 * x_N >= 0, "OldCompanyShare"

    # Solve using CBC (default solver in PuLP)
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    obj_value = pl.value(prob.objective)
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {obj_value}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
