[PLANNER]:
1. **Key optimisation goal**

- **Goal:** Minimise total meat used to produce small and large bones.

2. **Suggested model type**

- **Model:** Linear Programming (LP)
- Single-period, production planning–type model.

3. **Main decision variables**

Define nonnegative decision variables such as:

- \(x_s\): number of **small bones** produced  
- \(x_l\): number of **large bones** produced  

(If needed, you can also introduce a variable for total bones to express the 50% requirement more flexibly, e.g. \(T = x_s + x_l\), but this is optional since the ratio constraint can be expressed directly.)

4. **Objective function structure**

- Each small bone uses 12 units of meat.  
- Each large bone uses 15 units of meat.

Total meat used:  
- \(12 \cdot x_s + 15 \cdot x_l\).

**Objective:** Minimise \(12 x_s + 15 x_l\).

5. **Key constraints to consider**

a) **Tooth medication availability**

- Total tooth medication used cannot exceed 2000 units.
- Each small bone uses 10 units of tooth medication.
- Each large bone uses 15 units of tooth medication.
- Constraint structure:  
  - Tooth-medication-use from small + large ≤ 2000.

b) **Minimum proportion of small bones**

- At least 50% of all bones produced must be small.
- This is a ratio constraint linking \(x_s\) and \(x_l\).
- Structure:  
  - \(x_s\) ≥ 0.5 × (total bones).  
  - Total bones = \(x_s + x_l\).  
  - Rearranged to a linear inequality relating \(x_s\) and \(x_l\).

c) **Minimum number of large bones**

- Hospital must make at least 30 large bones.
- Constraint:  
  - \(x_l\) ≥ 30.

d) **Nonnegativity and integrality considerations**

- \(x_s, x_l\) ≥ 0.
- The problem is naturally integer (numbers of bones), but:
  - First model as an LP with continuous variables.
  - If the solution needs to be integer, either:
    - Round and check feasibility/cost, or
    - Formulate it explicitly as an Integer Linear Program (ILP).

6. **Modelling guidance / simplifications**

- There is no explicit upper bound on meat; meat is minimized implicitly.
- Tooth medication is the only hard capacity constraint.
- No upper bound on total bones except what is imposed by tooth medication.
- Check corner/feasible-region logic:  
  - Expect the tooth medication constraint and either the proportion constraint or the minimum-large-bones constraint to be binding at optimum.
- Once the model is formulated, solve via standard LP solver, then:
  - Verify that all constraints are satisfied (especially the 50% small-bone rule).
  - Confirm meat usage is minimized.

This structure gives a compact LP:  
- 2 decision variables  
- 3 main constraints (resource capacity, proportion requirement, minimum large bones) + nonnegativity.

[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical model for the problem.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_s \ge 0\): number of small bones produced  
- \(x_l \ge 0\): number of large bones produced  

(If integer counts are required, add \(x_s, x_l \in \mathbb{Z}\).)

---

## 2. Objective function

Each bone uses meat:

- Small bone: 12 units of meat  
- Large bone: 15 units of meat  

Total meat used:
\[
M = 12x_s + 15x_l
\]

**Objective:**  
\[
\min\; 12x_s + 15x_l
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Tooth medication capacity

Total tooth medication available: 2000 units.

- Small bone uses 10 units  
- Large bone uses 15 units  

So:
\[
10x_s + 15x_l \le 2000
\]

---

### b) At least 50% of bones must be small

Small bones must be at least half of all bones:

\[
x_s \ge 0.5(x_s + x_l)
\]

Rearrange to a linear form:

\[
x_s \ge \frac{1}{2}x_s + \frac{1}{2}x_l
\]

\[
x_s - \frac{1}{2}x_s \ge \frac{1}{2}x_l
\]

\[
\frac{1}{2}x_s \ge \frac{1}{2}x_l
\]

\[
x_s \ge x_l
\]

So you can use simply:
\[
x_s \ge x_l
\]

---

### c) Minimum number of large bones

At least 30 large bones must be made:

\[
x_l \ge 30
\]

---

### d) Nonnegativity

\[
x_s \ge 0,\quad x_l \ge 0
\]

(Optionally, integrality: \(x_s, x_l \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\).)

---

## 4. Full model (LP form)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min\quad & 12x_s + 15x_l \\
\text{s.t.}\quad
& 10x_s + 15x_l \le 2000 \\
& x_s \ge x_l \\
& x_l \ge 30 \\
& x_s \ge 0,\; x_l \ge 0
\end{aligned}
\]

If integer solutions are needed, add \(x_s, x_l \in \mathbb{Z}\).

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small linear (or integer linear) program. Good choices:

- **PuLP**: simple LP/ILP modeling, works with CBC (default), GLPK, etc.
- **OR-Tools (Google)**: `ortools.linear_solver` for LP/MIP.
- **SciPy**: `scipy.optimize.linprog` for continuous LP (no integrality).
- **Pyomo**: more general modeling, supports many solvers.

For integer counts, prefer **PuLP**, **OR-Tools**, or **Pyomo** with a MILP solver (CBC, GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc.).

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: minimize objective
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Bone_Production", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables (integer, nonnegative)
    x_s = pulp.LpVariable('x_s', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # small bones
    x_l = pulp.LpVariable('x_l', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # large bones

    # Objective function: minimize meat usage
    prob += 12 * x_s + 15 * x_l

    # Constraints
    # Tooth medication capacity
    prob += 10 * x_s + 15 * x_l <= 2000

    # At least 50% of bones must be small: x_s >= x_l
    prob += x_s >= x_l

    # Minimum number of large bones
    prob += x_l >= 30

    # Solve
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get objective value
    obj_value = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {obj_value}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
