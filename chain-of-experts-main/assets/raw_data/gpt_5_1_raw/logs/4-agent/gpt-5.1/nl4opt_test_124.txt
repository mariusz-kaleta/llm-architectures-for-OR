[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
Goal: Minimise the total number of drills used per day while meeting gem-processing requirements and respecting water and composition constraints.

2. Suggested model type  
Model: Integer Linear Programming (ILP).  
Reason: Number of drills must be integer and constraints/objective are linear.

3. Main decision variables  
Define decision variables at the “number of drills of each type” level, not per individual drill:

- Let:
  - HighDrills = number of high intensity drills used per day (integer ≥ 0)
  - LowDrills  = number of low intensity drills used per day (integer ≥ 0)

(Equivalently, you could name them x_H and x_L.)

4. Key constraints to consider  

a) Gem processing requirement (capacity constraint)  
- Each high intensity drill processes 50 gems/day.  
- Each low intensity drill processes 30 gems/day.  
- The factory must process at least 800 gems per day.  
→ Constraint: Total gems processed by all high + low drills must be ≥ 800.

b) Water availability constraint  
- Each high intensity drill uses 50 units of water/day.  
- Each low intensity drill uses 20 units of water/day.  
- The factory has at most 700 units of water available per day.  
→ Constraint: Total water used by all drills must be ≤ 700.

c) Composition constraint: share of high intensity drills  
- “At most 40% of the drills can be high intensity ones.”  
- This is a ratio constraint: HighDrills ≤ 0.4 × (HighDrills + LowDrills).  
→ Model this as a linear inequality by moving terms to one side; no need for extra variables.

d) Minimum number of low intensity drills  
- “At least 10 should be low intensity drills.”  
→ LowDrills ≥ 10.

e) Non-negativity and integrality  
- HighDrills, LowDrills ≥ 0.  
- Both must be integers (since you can’t use fractional drills).

5. Objective function  
- Minimise total number of drills: HighDrills + LowDrills.

6. Modelling strategy / notes for the Modeller  

- Formulate all constraints linearly in terms of HighDrills and LowDrills.  
- Treat the “at most 40% high intensity” condition carefully as a linear inequality involving both variables.  
- Enforce integrality on both decision variables; this is a 2-variable ILP and will be straightforward to solve exactly.  
- After solving, verify:
  - Gem capacity ≥ 800.  
  - Water use ≤ 700.  
  - Ratio of high drills ≤ 40%.  
  - LowDrills ≥ 10.  

No additional variables or binary indicators are strictly necessary for this problem.

[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem, followed by solver suggestions.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(H \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of high-intensity drills used per day  
- \(L \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of low-intensity drills used per day  

Both must be integers.

---

## 2. Objective

Minimise the total number of drills used:

\[
\min \; Z = H + L
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Gem-processing requirement

Each high drill: 50 gems/day  
Each low drill: 30 gems/day  

Must process **at least** 800 gems per day:

\[
50H + 30L \;\ge\; 800
\]

---

### b) Water availability

Each high drill: 50 water units/day  
Each low drill: 20 water units/day  

Available water: **at most** 700 units/day:

\[
50H + 20L \;\le\; 700
\]

---

### c) Composition: at most 40% high-intensity drills

“At most 40% of the drills can be high intensity”:

\[
\frac{H}{H + L} \;\le\; 0.4
\quad\text{for } H + L > 0
\]

Linearise by multiplying both sides by \(H + L\):

\[
H \;\le\; 0.4(H + L)
\]

\[
H \;\le\; 0.4H + 0.4L
\]

\[
H - 0.4H \;\le\; 0.4L
\]

\[
0.6H \;\le\; 0.4L
\]

Multiply by 5 to clear decimals:

\[
3H \;\le\; 2L
\]

This is a linear constraint.

*(If you want to be fully rigorous about the case \(H+L = 0\): the objective minimises \(H+L\) and the gem constraint forces some positive number of drills, so the optimal solution will have \(H+L>0\) anyway.)*

---

### d) Minimum number of low-intensity drills

“At least 10 should be low intensity drills”:

\[
L \;\ge\; 10
\]

---

### e) Non-negativity and integrality

\[
H \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0},\quad L \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Full ILP model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & H + L \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 50H + 30L \;\ge\; 800 &\text{(gems)}\\
& 50H + 20L \;\le\; 700 &\text{(water)}\\
& 3H \;\le\; 2L &\text{(≤ 40% high drills)}\\
& L \;\ge\; 10 &\text{(min low drills)}\\
& H, L \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any standard MILP (mixed-integer linear programming) / ILP library is appropriate:

- **PuLP**  
  - Very simple high-level modeling; can use CBC (open-source), GLPK, or commercial solvers.
  - Good for small ILPs like this.

- **OR-Tools (Google OR-Tools, `ortools.linear_solver`)**  
  - Efficient and well-maintained; supports integer variables, multiple solvers.

- **Pyomo**  
  - More general modeling environment, good if you plan to expand the model later.

- **cvxpy** with a MILP-capable backend (e.g., GLPK_MI, CBC, Gurobi, CPLEX)  
  - Nice high-level interface, but MILP support depends on the chosen solver.

For this specific two-variable ILP, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** is more than sufficient and straightforward.

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: minimization
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Drill_Optimization", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: non-negative integers
    H = pulp.LpVariable("H", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")
    L = pulp.LpVariable("L", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")

    # Objective: minimize total number of drills
    prob += H + L

    # Constraints

    # Gem-processing requirement: 50H + 30L >= 800
    prob += 50 * H + 30 * L >= 800, "GemRequirement"

    # Water availability: 50H + 20L <= 700
    prob += 50 * H + 20 * L <= 700, "WaterConstraint"

    # Composition: at most 40% high-intensity drills -> 3H <= 2L
    prob += 3 * H <= 2 * L, "HighDrillShare"

    # Minimum low-intensity drills: L >= 10
    prob += L >= 10, "MinLowDrills"

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print the final objective in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
