1. Key optimisation goal  
Goal: Minimise the total machine running time across both labs (i.e., minimise total lab hours), while satisfying pill production requirements and worker-hour limits.

2. Suggested model type  
Model: Linear Programming (LP) with two continuous decision variables (lab operating hours).

3. Main decision variables  
Define continuous variables (nonnegative):

- \(x_1\): Number of hours lab 1 is run.
- \(x_2\): Number of hours lab 2 is run.

4. Key constraints to consider  

a) Production capacity constraints  
Use pill outputs per hour at each lab:

- Heart medication requirement:  
  - Lab 1 produces 20 heart pills per hour.  
  - Lab 2 produces 30 heart pills per hour.  
  - Total heart pills must be at least 20,000.  
  → Constraint linking \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) to meet the minimum heart pill production.

- Lung medication requirement:  
  - Lab 1 produces 30 lung pills per hour.  
  - Lab 2 produces 40 lung pills per hour.  
  - Total lung pills must be at least 30,000.  
  → Constraint linking \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) to meet the minimum lung pill production.

b) Worker-hours constraint  
Each hour of lab operation corresponds to a “session” with a specific worker-hour requirement:

- Lab 1: 3 worker hours per hour of operation.
- Lab 2: 5 worker hours per hour of operation.
- Total available worker hours: 1,500.

Create a total worker-hour constraint combining \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) and ensure it does not exceed 1,500.

c) Non-negativity constraints  

- \(x_1 \ge 0\)  
- \(x_2 \ge 0\)

5. Objective function structure  

Objective: Minimise total lab operating time:

- Total time = \(x_1 + x_2\)

No need to include worker hours in the objective, as they are limited via a constraint; the objective focuses purely on minimising total operating hours of the labs.

Here is a clean mathematical optimisation model for the problem, with all elements laid out.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_1 \ge 0\): hours that lab 1 is operated  
- \(x_2 \ge 0\): hours that lab 2 is operated  

These are continuous (real-valued) variables.

---

## 2. Objective

Minimise total lab operating time (sum of hours across both labs):

\[
\min \; Z = x_1 + x_2
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Heart medication requirement

Lab 1 produces 20 heart pills/hour, lab 2 produces 30 heart pills/hour.  
At least 20,000 heart pills are required:

\[
20 x_1 + 30 x_2 \;\ge\; 20{,}000
\]

### b) Lung medication requirement

Lab 1 produces 30 lung pills/hour, lab 2 produces 40 lung pills/hour.  
At least 30,000 lung pills are required:

\[
30 x_1 + 40 x_2 \;\ge\; 30{,}000
\]

### c) Worker-hours constraint

- Lab 1: 3 worker-hours per hour of operation  
- Lab 2: 5 worker-hours per hour of operation  
- Total available worker-hours: 1,500

So:

\[
3 x_1 + 5 x_2 \;\le\; 1{,}500
\]

### d) Non-negativity

\[
x_1 \;\ge\; 0, \quad x_2 \;\ge\; 0
\]

---

## 4. Full LP formulation

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & x_1 + x_2 \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 20 x_1 + 30 x_2 \;\ge\; 20{,}000 \quad &\text{(heart pills)}\\
& 30 x_1 + 40 x_2 \;\ge\; 30{,}000 \quad &\text{(lung pills)}\\
& 3 x_1 + 5 x_2 \;\le\; 1{,}500 \quad &\text{(worker hours)}\\
& x_1, x_2 \;\ge\; 0 \quad &\text{(non-negativity)}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is a standard linear program (LP) with two variables and three functional constraints.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any linear programming-capable library will work. Common, accessible choices:

- **PuLP**  
  - High-level modeling, pure Python, easy to install.  
  - Solver can be CBC (bundled), GLPK, or others.
- **SciPy** (`scipy.optimize.linprog`)  
  - Good for small to medium LPs, easy to use; constraints must be put into \(\le\) form.
- **OR-Tools** (Google OR-Tools, `ortools.linear_solver`)  
  - Industrial-strength, supports many solvers (CBC, GLOP, etc.), good performance and flexibility.
- **Pyomo**  
  - General algebraic modeling language in Python; good if you’ll scale up or extend to more complex models.

For simplicity and readability for this two-variable LP, **PuLP** or **SciPy’s `linprog`** are very natural choices.

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create LP problem: minimize total operating hours
    model = pulp.LpProblem("Lab_Operation_Time_Minimization", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: hours of operation for each lab (non-negative)
    x1 = pulp.LpVariable('x1', lowBound=0)  # hours lab 1
    x2 = pulp.LpVariable('x2', lowBound=0)  # hours lab 2

    # Objective: minimize x1 + x2
    model += x1 + x2

    # Constraints

    # Heart medication requirement: 20 x1 + 30 x2 >= 20000
    model += 20 * x1 + 30 * x2 >= 20000, "Heart_pills"

    # Lung medication requirement: 30 x1 + 40 x2 >= 30000
    model += 30 * x1 + 40 * x2 >= 30000, "Lung_pills"

    # Worker-hours constraint: 3 x1 + 5 x2 <= 1500
    model += 3 * x1 + 5 * x2 <= 1500, "Worker_hours"

    # Solve the model
    model.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get objective value
    obj_value = pulp.value(model.objective)

    # Print final objective in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {obj_value}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
