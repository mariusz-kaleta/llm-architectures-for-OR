[MODELER]:
Here is a complete linear programming (LP) formulation of the problem.

### Decision variables

Let  
- \(x \ge 0\): number of servings of noodles per day  
- \(y \ge 0\): number of servings of protein bars per day  

Both are continuous and nonnegative.

---

### Objective function

Minimize total daily cost (in dollars):

\[
\min \; Z = 5x + 2.5y
\]

where:
- \(5\) = cost per serving of noodles
- \(2.5\) = cost per serving of protein bar

---

### Constraints

1. **Calorie requirement**

Each serving of noodles has 600 calories, each protein bar has 250 calories, and Bob needs at least 2000 calories:

\[
600x + 250y \;\ge\; 2000
\]

2. **Protein requirement**

Each serving of noodles has 1.5 g of protein, each protein bar has 5 g of protein, and Bob needs at least 16 g of protein:

\[
1.5x + 5y \;\ge\; 16
\]

3. **Nonnegativity**

\[
x \ge 0,\quad y \ge 0
\]

---

### Compact LP model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min\ & 5x + 2.5y \\
\text{s.t. } 
& 600x + 250y \ge 2000 \\
& 1.5x + 5y \ge 16 \\
& x \ge 0,\ y \ge 0
\end{aligned}
\]

import pulp

def main():
    # Create the LP problem: minimize cost
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Diet_Problem", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: x = servings of noodles, y = servings of protein bars
    x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Continuous')
    y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Continuous')

    # Objective function: Minimize 5x + 2.5y
    prob += 5 * x + 2.5 * y

    # Constraints
    # Calorie requirement: 600x + 250y >= 2000
    prob += 600 * x + 250 * y >= 2000, "CalorieRequirement"

    # Protein requirement: 1.5x + 5y >= 16
    prob += 1.5 * x + 5 * y >= 16, "ProteinRequirement"

    # Solve the LP
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get the optimal objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print final objective as required
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
