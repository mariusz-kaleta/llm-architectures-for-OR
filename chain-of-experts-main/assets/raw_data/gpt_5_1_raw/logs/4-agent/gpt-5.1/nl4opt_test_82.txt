[PLANNER]:
1. **Key optimisation goal**

- **Goal:** Maximize total tips earned from all monthly shifts.

2. **Suggested model type**

- **Model:** Linear Programming (LP) model.  
  - Objective and all constraints are linear in the number of bike and scooter shifts.
  - If needed, you can treat the number of shifts as integer variables, but solving the LP relaxation and then checking integer feasibility is straightforward because there are only two variables.

3. **Main decision variables**

Define two primary decision variables:

- \( x_B \): Number of **bike shifts** scheduled in the month.
- \( x_S \): Number of **scooter shifts** scheduled in the month.

Both should be constrained to be nonnegative, and in an applied implementation, integer.

4. **Objective function structure**

- Tips per bike shift: **\$50**  
- Tips per scooter shift: **\$43**

Objective:  
- Maximize total tips = \( 50 \cdot x_B + 43 \cdot x_S \).

5. **Key constraints to consider**

a) **Total shifts limit**

- The person has **40 shifts** available in the month.
- Constraint: Total shifts used cannot exceed (and presumably will not need to be less than) this:
  - \( x_B + x_S \le 40 \).

b) **Energy capacity constraint**

- Bike shift uses **5 units** of energy.
- Scooter shift uses **6 units** of energy.
- Total energy available: **230 units**.
- Constraint on total energy consumption:
  - \( 5 \cdot x_B + 6 \cdot x_S \le 230 \).

c) **Minimum orders requirement**

- Bike shift delivers **10 orders**.
- Scooter shift delivers **7 orders**.
- At least **320 orders** must be delivered.
- Orders constraint:
  - \( 10 \cdot x_B + 7 \cdot x_S \ge 320 \).

d) **Minimum scooter shifts**

- Must have at least **5 shifts on scooter**:
  - \( x_S \ge 5 \).

e) **Nonnegativity and integrality**

- Nonnegativity:
  - \( x_B \ge 0 \),
  - \( x_S \ge 0 \).
- In practice, shifts are discrete, so:
  - \( x_B, x_S \) should be integers.

6. **Modelling notes / strategy**

- Start with a basic LP formulation using continuous variables to:
  - Define the feasible region by intersecting:
    - total shifts,
    - energy constraint,
    - orders minimum,
    - scooter minimum,
    - nonnegativity.
- Because there are only two variables, you can:
  - Solve graphically (plot constraints and find the feasible polygon and corner points), or
  - Use a standard LP solver and then adjust to the nearest integer feasible solution if needed.
- Compare the objective values at all **corner (extreme) points** of the feasible region; the best one gives the optimal mix of bike vs scooter shifts.
- After obtaining the LP optimum:
  - If results are non-integer, search nearby integer points that satisfy all constraints and select the one with maximum tips.

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of **bike shifts** in the month  
- \(x_S \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of **scooter shifts** in the month  

(If you want a pure LP relaxation, you can drop the integrality and keep them continuous.)

---

## 2. Objective function

Each shift yields tips:

- Bike shift: \$50  
- Scooter shift: \$43  

Maximize total tips:
\[
\max\ Z = 50\,x_B + 43\,x_S
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Total shifts limit

At most 40 shifts in the month:
\[
x_B + x_S \le 40
\]

### b) Energy capacity

Each shift consumes energy:

- Bike: 5 units  
- Scooter: 6 units  

Total energy available is 230 units:
\[
5x_B + 6x_S \le 230
\]

### c) Minimum orders requirement

Each shift delivers orders:

- Bike: 10 orders  
- Scooter: 7 orders  

Must deliver **at least 320 orders**:
\[
10x_B + 7x_S \ge 320
\]

### d) Minimum scooter shifts

At least 5 scooter shifts:
\[
x_S \ge 5
\]

### e) Nonnegativity and integrality

\[
x_B \ge 0,\quad x_S \ge 0,\quad x_B, x_S \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

---

## 4. Full model (MIP form)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max\quad & 50x_B + 43x_S \\
\text{s.t.}\quad 
& x_B + x_S \le 40, \\
& 5x_B + 6x_S \le 230, \\
& 10x_B + 7x_S \ge 320, \\
& x_S \ge 5, \\
& x_B \ge 0,\ x_S \ge 0, \\
& x_B, x_S \in \mathbb{Z}.
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any linear / mixed-integer programming solver works well here:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`) – simple interface, uses CBC by default, great for teaching/small problems.  
- **OR-Tools Linear Solver** (`ortools.linear_solver.pywraplp`) – robust, good for MIPs.  
- **SciPy** (`scipy.optimize.linprog`) – good for LP relaxation (continuous variables only); you’d need to handle integrality by rounding/search around the LP optimum.

Because this is naturally an integer problem (numbers of shifts), I recommend:

- **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** if you want exact integer solutions directly.

## 6. Example model in PuLP (sketch)

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Delivery_Shift_Planning", pl.LpMaximize)

# Variables (integer)
x_B = pl.LpVariable("x_B", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")  # bike shifts
x_S = pl.LpVariable("x_S", lowBound=5, cat="Integer")  # scooter shifts, min 5

# Objective
prob += 50 * x_B + 43 * x_S

# Constraints
prob += x_B + x_S <= 40                     # total shifts
prob += 5 * x_B + 6 * x_S <= 230           # energy
prob += 10 * x_B + 7 * x_S >= 320          # orders

# Solve
prob.solve()

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Bike shifts:", pl.value(x_B))
print("Scooter shifts:", pl.value(x_S))
print("Total tips:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

This fully encodes the mathematical model described above and will return the optimal number of bike and scooter shifts to maximize tips under all constraints.

[CODER]:
import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Define the problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Delivery_Shift_Planning", pl.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables
    x_B = pl.LpVariable("x_B", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")   # bike shifts
    x_S = pl.LpVariable("x_S", lowBound=5, cat="Integer")   # scooter shifts, min 5

    # Objective function: Maximize total tips
    prob += 50 * x_B + 43 * x_S

    # Constraints
    prob += x_B + x_S <= 40                 # total shifts limit
    prob += 5 * x_B + 6 * x_S <= 230        # energy capacity
    prob += 10 * x_B + 7 * x_S >= 320       # minimum orders

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract final objective value
    final_obj = pl.value(prob.objective)

    # Print the final objective value in required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
