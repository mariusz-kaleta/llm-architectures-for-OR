[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
   - Goal: Minimise total pollution generated by transporting at least 70 visitors to the top, subject to operational limits.

2. Suggested model type  
   - Model: Integer Linear Programming (ILP)  
     (Balloon and gondola rides are naturally counted as whole numbers.)

3. Main decision variables  
   - Number of hot-air balloon rides to operate.  
   - Number of gondola lift rides to operate.

4. Objective function (conceptual)  
   - Minimise total pollution:  
     - Pollution per balloon ride = 10 units.  
     - Pollution per gondola ride = 15 units.  
   - Total pollution = 10 × (number of balloon rides) + 15 × (number of gondola rides).

5. Key constraints  

   a) Capacity (visitor transport) constraint  
   - Each balloon ride carries 4 visitors.  
   - Each gondola ride carries 6 visitors.  
   - Total visitors transported must be at least 70:  
     - 4 × (balloon rides) + 6 × (gondola rides) ≥ 70.

   b) Operational limit on balloons  
   - At most 10 hot-air balloon rides are allowed:  
     - (balloon rides) ≤ 10.

   c) Non-negativity and integrality  
   - Numbers of rides cannot be negative.  
   - Both numbers must be integers (you cannot have fractional rides).

6. Modelling notes / strategy tips for the Modeller  

   - Units: Treat one “ride” as the unit of the decision variable.  
   - Integrality is important: feasible solutions should be integer-valued.  
   - Search strategy:  
     - Because one variable has a tight upper bound (balloon rides ≤ 10), enumerating balloon counts 0–10 and solving for the minimum feasible gondola rides for each is easy and can be used to verify the ILP solution.  
   - Interpretation of results:  
     - Once an optimal combination (balloon rides, gondola rides) is found, verify:  
       - Visitor capacity ≥ 70.  
       - Balloon rides ≤ 10.  
       - Total pollution is minimal among all integer-feasible combinations.

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of hot‑air balloon rides  
- \(y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of gondola lift rides  

(We count rides, not individual passengers.)

---

## 2. Objective function

Pollution per ride:

- Balloon: 10 units per ride  
- Gondola: 15 units per ride  

Minimise total pollution:

\[
\text{Minimise } Z = 10x + 15y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Visitor capacity constraint

Each balloon carries 4 visitors, each gondola 6 visitors. At least 70 visitors must be transported:

\[
4x + 6y \;\ge\; 70
\]

### b) Operational limit on balloons

At most 10 balloon rides:

\[
x \;\le\; 10
\]

### c) Non‑negativity and integrality

\[
x \ge 0,\; y \ge 0,\quad x, y \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

---

## 4. Model summary (ILP)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min_{x,y} \quad & 10x + 15y \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 4x + 6y \ge 70 \\
& x \le 10 \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is a small Integer Linear Programming problem.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

Any of these can express and solve this ILP directly:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`) – very simple syntax, uses CBC or other MILP solvers.
- **OR-Tools Linear Solver** (`ortools.linear_solver.pywraplp`) – Google’s MILP/LP toolkit.
- **Pyomo** – more general algebraic modelling, can use CBC, GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc.

For this size, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** is ideal.

---

## 6. Example PuLP model

```python
import pulp as pl

# Create the problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Mountain_Transport_Pollution_Min", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables (non-negative integers)
x = pl.LpVariable('balloon_rides', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
y = pl.LpVariable('gondola_rides', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective: minimize total pollution
prob += 10 * x + 15 * y, "Total_Pollution"

# Constraints
prob += 4 * x + 6 * y >= 70, "Visitor_Capacity"
prob += x <= 10, "Balloon_Limit"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Optimal balloon rides (x):", pl.value(x))
print("Optimal gondola rides (y):", pl.value(y))
print("Minimum pollution:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

You can analogously implement this in OR-Tools if you prefer.

import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Create the problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Mountain_Transport_Pollution_Min", pl.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables (non-negative integers)
    x = pl.LpVariable('balloon_rides', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    y = pl.LpVariable('gondola_rides', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective: minimize total pollution
    prob += 10 * x + 15 * y, "Total_Pollution"

    # Constraints
    prob += 4 * x + 6 * y >= 70, "Visitor_Capacity"
    prob += x <= 10, "Balloon_Limit"

    # Solve with CBC
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract final objective value
    final_obj = pl.value(prob.objective)

    # Required final print
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
