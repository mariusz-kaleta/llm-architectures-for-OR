1. Key optimisation goal  
   - Goal: Minimise the **total daily water** required to hydrate all soil bags used in the garden bed.

2. Suggested model type  
   - Model: **Linear Programming (LP)** with two decision variables and linear constraints.

3. Main decision variables  
   Define variables as integer counts of bags (the modeller can later decide whether to relax integrality if appropriate):
   - \(x_s\): Number of **subsoil** bags used.
   - \(x_t\): Number of **topsoil** bags used.

   Notes for modelling:
   - Objective coefficients:  
     - Subsoil: 10 units of water per bag per day.  
     - Topsoil: 6 units of water per bag per day.
   - Combined capacity: There can be at most 150 bags in total (subsoil + topsoil).

4. Key constraints to consider  

   a. Capacity / availability of total bags  
   - The total number of bags (subsoil + topsoil) cannot exceed the truck capacity / available stock:
     - \(x_s + x_t \leq 150\).

   b. Minimum topsoil requirement  
   - At least 10 bags of topsoil must be used:
     - \(x_t \geq 10\).

   c. Maximum share of topsoil (cost-related composition constraint)  
   - At most 30% of all soil bags can be topsoil:
     - \(x_t \leq 0.30 \times (x_s + x_t)\).  
   - The modeller should convert this to an equivalent linear inequality without division to keep the LP in standard form.

   d. Nonnegativity and integrality  
   - Both variables must be nonnegative:
     - \(x_s \geq 0\), \(x_t \geq 0\).
   - In a realistic setting, bag counts must be integers (use integer or mixed-integer programming); for simple textbook treatment, they may be left as continuous and rounded to feasible integers afterward if necessary.

5. Objective function structure  

   - Minimise total daily water usage:
     - Total water = \(10 \times x_s + 6 \times x_t\).  
   - The modeller should set this as the LP objective function.

6. Modelling strategy / key checks for the Modeller  

   - Ensure that the “at most 30% topsoil” constraint is **correctly linearised** (no division by decision variables).  
   - Observe that the objective will push the solution toward using more of the soil type with **lower water coefficient** per bag, while the composition and minimum-topsoil constraints will bound that tendency.
   - After solving the LP, check:
     - That all constraints are satisfied (especially the 30% rule).
     - That the solution respects integrality (if using integer programming); if relaxed, adjust as needed to the nearest feasible integer point and compare water usage.

This structure provides everything required for the Modeller to write and solve the LP/MIP to obtain the optimal numbers of subsoil and topsoil bags.

Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem, with all pieces identified and ready to implement.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(x_s \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of subsoil bags purchased.  
- \(x_t \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of topsoil bags purchased.

(You may relax to \(x_s, x_t \ge 0\) real for a pure LP.)

---

## 2. Objective function

Minimise the total daily water needed to hydrate all bags:

\[
\min \; Z = 10\,x_s + 6\,x_t
\]

- Each subsoil bag uses 10 units of water per day.  
- Each topsoil bag uses 6 units of water per day.

---

## 3. Constraints

### (a) Total capacity of bags

The total number of bags the truck can carry is at most 150:

\[
x_s + x_t \le 150
\]

---

### (b) Minimum topsoil requirement

At least 10 bags of topsoil must be used:

\[
x_t \ge 10
\]

---

### (c) At most 30% of all bags are topsoil

Text: “At most 30% of all bags of soil can be topsoil.”

Start from:

\[
x_t \le 0.30 \,(x_s + x_t)
\]

Eliminate the division / variable in denominator (linearisation):

\[
\begin{aligned}
x_t &\le 0.30 x_s + 0.30 x_t \\
x_t - 0.30 x_t &\le 0.30 x_s \\
0.70 x_t &\le 0.30 x_s \\
7x_t &\le 3x_s \quad\text{(multiplying both sides by 10)}
\end{aligned}
\]

So the linear constraint in standard form is:

\[
7x_t \le 3x_s
\]

This is the key composition constraint.

---

### (d) Nonnegativity and integrality

\[
x_s \ge 0,\quad x_t \ge 0,\quad x_s, x_t \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

If you treat it as a standard LP (continuous), just drop integrality:

\[
x_s, x_t \ge 0
\]

---

## 4. Complete model (MIP form)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Minimise} \quad & Z = 10x_s + 6x_t \\
\text{subject to} \quad 
& x_s + x_t \le 150, \\
& x_t \ge 10, \\
& 7x_t \le 3x_s, \\
& x_s, x_t \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}.
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Notes on solution structure

- Water per bag:
  - Subsoil: 10
  - Topsoil: 6 (better, uses less water)
- The objective “wants” more topsoil (6 < 10) but:
  - We must respect that topsoil is at most 30% of total bags (7x_t ≤ 3x_s).
  - We must also use at least 10 topsoil bags (x_t ≥ 10).
- Capacity 150 may or may not bind at optimum; the solver will determine this.

## 6. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small linear / mixed-integer optimisation problem. Any standard LP/MIP library works well:

- **PuLP** (pure Python, easy to use; uses CBC by default for MIP)
- **OR-Tools** (from Google; `pywraplp` / `linear_solver` or CP-SAT)
- **Pyomo** (more general modelling environment; can call CBC, GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc.)

## 7. Example PuLP formulation (MIP)

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("Soil_Water_Min", pl.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables (integer)
x_s = pl.LpVariable("x_s", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")  # subsoil bags
x_t = pl.LpVariable("x_t", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")  # topsoil bags

# Objective
prob += 10 * x_s + 6 * x_t, "Total_Water"

# Constraints
prob += x_s + x_t <= 150,          "Capacity"
prob += x_t >= 10,                 "Min_Topsoil"
prob += 7 * x_t <= 3 * x_s,        "Max_30pct_Topsoil"

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

# Results
print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Subsoil bags (x_s):", pl.value(x_s))
print("Topsoil bags (x_t):", pl.value(x_t))
print("Min total water:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

You can adapt the same mathematical model to OR-Tools or Pyomo with analogous variable, objective, and constraint definitions.

import pulp as pl

def main():
    # Define the problem
    prob = pl.LpProblem("Soil_Water_Min", pl.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables (integer)
    x_s = pl.LpVariable("x_s", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")  # subsoil bags
    x_t = pl.LpVariable("x_t", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")  # topsoil bags

    # Objective function: minimize total daily water
    prob += 10 * x_s + 6 * x_t, "Total_Water"

    # Constraints
    prob += x_s + x_t <= 150,   "Capacity"
    prob += x_t >= 10,          "Min_Topsoil"
    prob += 7 * x_t <= 3 * x_s, "Max_30pct_Topsoil"

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Extract the objective value

if __name__ == "__main__":
