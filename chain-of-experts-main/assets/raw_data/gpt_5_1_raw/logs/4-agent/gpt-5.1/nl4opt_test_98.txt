[PLANNER]:
1. Key optimisation goal  
   - Goal: Minimise the **total transport time** required to move at least 120 patients to the hospital.  
   - Total time = (number of helicopter trips × 1 hour) + (number of bus trips × 3 hours).

2. Suggested model type  
   - Model: **Integer Linear Programming (ILP)**.  
     - Decision variables are naturally counts of trips, so they must be integers.  
     - Objective and constraints are all linear in those variables.

3. Main decision variables  
   Define integer, non-negative variables such as:  
   - H = number of helicopter trips.  
   - B = number of bus trips.  

   (If needed later, you can derive number of patients transported by each mode as 5H and 8B.)

4. Key constraints to consider  

   a) Patient coverage requirement  
   - Each helicopter trip transports 5 patients.  
   - Each bus trip transports 8 patients.  
   - At least 120 patients must be transported:  
     - 5H + 8B ≥ 120.  

   b) Minimum helicopter share of trips  
   - “At least 30% of the trips should be by helicopter.”  
   - This is about **number of trips**, not patients:  
     - H ≥ 0.3 × (H + B).  
   - This will be a linear constraint once rearranged.

   c) Maximum number of bus trips  
   - At most 10 bus trips are allowed:  
     - B ≤ 10.  

   d) Non-negativity and integrality  
   - H ≥ 0, B ≥ 0.  
   - H and B must be **integers** (number of trips cannot be fractional).

5. Objective function structure  

   - Minimise total time T:  
     - T = 1 × H + 3 × B.  
   - This is linear in the decision variables.

6. Modelling strategy notes for the Modeller  

   - Keep the model very compact: 2 integer variables, 3–4 linear constraints, 1 linear objective.  
   - Explicitly enforce integrality; otherwise, the LP relaxation might yield fractional trips.  
   - After solving, verify:  
     - Total patients transported ≥ 120.  
     - H/(H + B) ≥ 0.3.  
     - B ≤ 10.  
   - If multiple optimal solutions exist, any one satisfying all constraints is acceptable unless the user later specifies secondary criteria (e.g., minimise number of trips or helicopters).

[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical optimisation model of the problem, followed by implementation notes.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let

- \(H \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of helicopter trips  
- \(B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\): number of bus trips  

Both are integer and non‑negative.

Derived (not decision) quantities:

- Patients by helicopter: \(5H\)  
- Patients by bus: \(8B\)  
- Total trips: \(H + B\)  
- Total patients: \(5H + 8B\)

---

## 2. Objective function

Minimise total transport time \(T\):

\[
\min \; T = 1 \cdot H + 3 \cdot B
\]

- 1 hour per helicopter trip  
- 3 hours per bus trip  

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Patient coverage requirement

At least 120 patients transported:

\[
5H + 8B \;\ge\; 120
\]

### b) Minimum helicopter share of trips

At least 30% of the trips must be by helicopter:

\[
\frac{H}{H + B} \;\ge\; 0.3 \quad \text{(when } H + B > 0\text{)}
\]

Rearrange to a linear form:

\[
H \;\ge\; 0.3(H + B)
\]
\[
H \;\ge\; 0.3H + 0.3B
\]
\[
0.7H \;\ge\; 0.3B
\]
\[
7H \;\ge\; 3B
\]

So the linear constraint is:

\[
7H - 3B \;\ge\; 0
\]

Note: Given 120 patients and integer trips, \(H + B > 0\) will be automatically satisfied by feasibility.

### c) Maximum number of bus trips

At most 10 bus trips:

\[
B \;\le\; 10
\]

### d) Non‑negativity and integrality

\[
H \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}, \quad B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Full ILP formulation

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & T = H + 3B \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 5H + 8B \;\ge\; 120 & \text{(patients)} \\
& 7H - 3B \;\ge\; 0 & \text{(≥30% helicopter trips)} \\
& B \;\le\; 10 & \text{(bus limit)} \\
& H, B \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0} & \text{(integrality, non-negativity)}
\end{aligned}
\]

Any optimal integer solution \((H^*, B^*)\) gives how many helicopter and bus trips to schedule.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small Integer Linear Program with two integer variables and a few linear constraints. Any standard MILP/LP solver is appropriate:

- `PuLP` (pure Python modeling, can use CBC by default; very suitable here)
- `pyomo` (more general modeling framework, supports many solvers)
- `ortools.linear_solver` from Google OR-Tools (MILP solver with CBC, SCIP, or GLOP)
- `scipy.optimize.linprog` **cannot** handle integrality directly, so it’s not ideal unless you relax integrality and then round/adjust (not recommended for exact solution here).

For a straightforward implementation:

- Use **PuLP**: simplest for teaching/demo.
- Or **OR-Tools** if you already use Google’s optimization tools.

[CODER]:
import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: minimize total transport time
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Helicopter_Bus_Transport", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: H = helicopter trips, B = bus trips
    H = pulp.LpVariable('H', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
    B = pulp.LpVariable('B', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

    # Objective function: minimize T = H + 3B
    prob += H + 3 * B, "Total_Transport_Time"

    # Constraints

    # At least 120 patients: 5H + 8B >= 120
    prob += 5 * H + 8 * B >= 120, "Patient_Coverage"

    # At least 30% of trips by helicopter: 7H - 3B >= 0
    prob += 7 * H - 3 * B >= 0, "Helicopter_Share"

    # At most 10 bus trips: B <= 10
    prob += B <= 10, "Bus_Limit"

    # Solve the problem
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get optimal objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print the final objective value in the required format
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
