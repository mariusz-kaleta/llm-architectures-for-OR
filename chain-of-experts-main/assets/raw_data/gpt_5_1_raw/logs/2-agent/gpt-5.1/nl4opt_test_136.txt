Here is a clean mathematical model of your problem.

---

### 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x\) = number of seasonal volunteers (integer, \(x \ge 0\))  
- \(y\) = number of full-time volunteers (integer, \(y \ge 0\))

---

### 2. Objective

Maximize the total number of gifts delivered.

- Seasonal volunteer delivers 5 gifts → total from seasonal = \(5x\)  
- Full-time volunteer delivers 8 gifts → total from full-time = \(8y\)

**Objective function:**
\[
\max \; Z = 5x + 8y
\]

---

### 3. Constraints

1. **Points budget constraint**

   - Seasonal volunteer gets 2 points → total points from seasonal = \(2x\)  
   - Full-time volunteer gets 5 points → total points from full-time = \(5y\)  
   - Total points cannot exceed 200:

   \[
   2x + 5y \le 200
   \]

2. **Seasonal volunteers at most 30% of all volunteers**

   Seasonal volunteers are at most 30% of total volunteers:

   \[
   x \le 0.3(x + y)
   \]

   Simplify:

   \[
   x \le 0.3x + 0.3y \\
   x - 0.3x \le 0.3y \\
   0.7x \le 0.3y \\
   7x \le 3y
   \]

3. **At least 10 full-time volunteers**

   \[
   y \ge 10
   \]

4. **Non-negativity and integrality**

   \[
   x \ge 0,\quad y \ge 0,\quad x, y \in \mathbb{Z}
   \]

---

### 4. Full model summary (Integer Linear Program)

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Maximize}\quad & 5x + 8y \\
\text{subject to}\quad
& 2x + 5y \le 200, \\
& 7x \le 3y, \\
& y \ge 10, \\
& x \ge 0,\; y \ge 0, \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}.
\end{aligned}
\]

This is an integer linear programming (ILP) problem.

---

### 5. Solving this in Python

Suitable libraries for solving this ILP include:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`): Very straightforward modeling of linear and integer programs.
- **OR-Tools** (`ortools.linear_solver`): Google’s optimization suite, good performance and flexibility.
- **Pyomo**: More general algebraic modeling language, good for larger or more complex models.
- **SciPy** (`scipy.optimize`) is not ideal here because it does not natively handle integer constraints; you’d need an external MILP solver.

---

### 6. Example with PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Define problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("ToyStoreVolunteers", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables
x = pl.LpVariable('seasonal', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')
y = pl.LpVariable('full_time', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')

# Objective
prob += 5 * x + 8 * y

# Constraints
prob += 2 * x + 5 * y <= 200           # points budget
prob += 7 * x <= 3 * y                 # seasonal <= 30% of total
prob += y >= 10                        # at least 10 full-time

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Seasonal volunteers (x):", pl.value(x))
print("Full-time volunteers (y):", pl.value(y))
print("Maximum gifts:", pl.value(5*x + 8*y))
```

This will give you the optimal numbers of seasonal and full-time volunteers to maximize the total gifts delivered under the given constraints.
