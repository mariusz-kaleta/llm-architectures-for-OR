[MODELER]:
Below is a linear programming (LP) model of the problem.

---

## 1. Decision Variables

Let  
- \( x \) = number of trips made by the **new** company  
- \( y \) = number of trips made by the **old** company  

We assume \( x \ge 0, y \ge 0 \) and, in reality, \(x\) and \(y\) should be integers (number of trips).

---

## 2. Objective

Minimize the **total diesel used**.

- New company: 30 liters per trip → total diesel \(= 30x\)
- Old company: 40 liters per trip → total diesel \(= 40y\)

**Objective function:**
\[
\min Z = 30x + 40y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1 Gift delivery requirement

Each trip by:
- New company delivers 50 gifts → \(50x\)
- Old company delivers 70 gifts → \(70y\)

At least 1000 gifts must be delivered:
\[
50x + 70y \;\ge\; 1000
\]

---

### 3.2 Maximum trips by the new company

At most 15 trips by the new company:
\[
x \;\le\; 15
\]

---

### 3.3 Minimum fraction of trips by the old company

“At least 40% of all trips must be made by the old company.”

Total trips = \(x + y\)  
Trips by old company = \(y\)

\[
\frac{y}{x + y} \;\ge\; 0.4 \quad\text{(for } x + y > 0\text{)}
\]

Multiply both sides by \(x + y\) (nonnegative):
\[
y \;\ge\; 0.4(x + y)
\]

\[
y \;\ge\; 0.4x + 0.4y
\]

\[
y - 0.4y \;\ge\; 0.4x
\]

\[
0.6y \;\ge\; 0.4x
\]

\[
3y \;\ge\; 2x
\]

So the linear constraint is:
\[
3y - 2x \;\ge\; 0
\]

---

### 3.4 Nonnegativity and integrality

\[
x \;\ge\; 0,\quad y \;\ge\; 0
\]

Since trips must be whole numbers:
\[
x, y \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

---

## 4. Full Model

\[
\begin{align}
\min\quad & Z = 30x + 40y \\
\text{s.t.}\quad
& 50x + 70y \;\ge\; 1000 \\
& x \;\le\; 15 \\
& 3y - 2x \;\ge\; 0 \\
& x, y \;\ge\; 0,\; x,y \in \mathbb{Z}
\end{align}
\]

This is a small mixed-integer linear program (MILP). Because coefficients are all integers and the problem is small, solving it as a pure LP (ignoring integrality) and then rounding/checking nearby integer points will often directly yield the integer optimum; but a MILP solver will handle it exactly.

---

## 5. Suitable Python Libraries

All of the following can solve this model:

- **PuLP**  
  - Simple modeling syntax, integrates with CBC (open-source MILP solver).
  - Good for teaching, small to medium problems.
  - Example usage: `pulp.LpProblem`, `pulp.LpVariable`, solve with CBC.

- **OR-Tools (Google OR-Tools)**  
  - Very good MILP engine (`pywraplp.Solver` with CBC or SCIP backends).
  - Efficient for larger integer programs as well.

- **Pyomo**  
  - More general algebraic modeling language in Python.
  - Can use CBC, GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc., as back-end solvers.

- **scipy.optimize**  
  - Has `linprog` for LP, but does **not** support integrality directly.
  - Suitable only if you relax \(x, y\) to be continuous and then handle integrality manually or accept fractional solutions.

For this type of discrete optimization (integer trips), **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are the most straightforward choices.

import pulp

def main():
    # Create the MILP problem
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Gift_Delivery", pulp.LpMinimize)

    # Decision variables: integer and nonnegative
    x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # trips by new company
    y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # trips by old company

    # Objective function: minimize total diesel
    prob += 30 * x + 40 * y

    # Constraints
    # Gift delivery requirement
    prob += 50 * x + 70 * y >= 1000

    # Max trips by new company
    prob += x <= 15

    # At least 40% of trips by old company: 3y - 2x >= 0
    prob += 3 * y - 2 * x >= 0

    # Solve the problem using default CBC solver
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Get the optimal objective value
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
