[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical optimization model of the problem.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x\) = number of wide pipes (integer, \(x \ge 0\))  
- \(y\) = number of narrow pipes (integer, \(y \ge 0\))

---

## 2. Objective

Minimize the total number of pipes:

\[
\min\; z = x + y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Water capacity requirement

Each wide pipe carries 25 units/min; each narrow pipe carries 15 units/min.  
Total water transported per minute must be at least 900 units:

\[
25x + 15y \;\ge\; 900
\]

### b) Wide pipes limited to at most a third of narrow pipes

“The number of wide pipes can be at most a third the number of narrow pipes”:

\[
x \;\le\; \frac{1}{3} y
\]

Equivalently (if you want to avoid fractions):

\[
3x \;\le\; y
\]

### c) Minimum number of wide pipes

“At least 5 wide pipes must be used”:

\[
x \;\ge\; 5
\]

### d) Integrality and nonnegativity

\[
x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Full model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\min\quad & x + y \\
\text{s.t.}\quad 
& 25x + 15y \;\ge\; 900 \\
& 3x \;\le\; y \\
& x \;\ge\; 5 \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{aligned}
\]

This is an **integer linear programming (ILP)** problem.

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

You can solve this with standard MILP/LP tools. Good options:

- **PuLP**  
  - Simple modeling interface, works with CBC (included), GLPK, Gurobi, CPLEX, etc.
  - Good for teaching and small/medium problems.

- **OR-Tools (Google)** – `ortools.linear_solver`  
  - Very efficient, especially with CBC or GLOP.
  - Good if you might expand to routing or scheduling later.

- **Pyomo**  
  - More general algebraic modeling language.
  - Good if you may grow the model or connect to different solvers.

- **SciPy (`scipy.optimize.linprog`)**  
  - Can solve the *continuous* relaxation (ignoring integrality).
  - Not ideal here if you need strictly integer solutions.

For this problem, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** is most straightforward.

---

[PROGRAMMER-VALIDATOR]:
import pulp

# Create the problem
prob = pulp.LpProblem("Pipe_Optimization", pulp.LpMinimize)

# Decision variables
x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # number of wide pipes
y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # number of narrow pipes

# Objective function: minimize total number of pipes
prob += x + y

# Constraints
prob += 25 * x + 15 * y >= 900          # water capacity requirement
prob += 3 * x <= y                      # wide pipes at most a third of narrow pipes
prob += x >= 5                          # at least 5 wide pipes

# Solve the problem
prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

# Extract solution
x_val = pulp.value(x)
y_val = pulp.value(y)
final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

# Print results (only the required final objective line as per instructions)
print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")
