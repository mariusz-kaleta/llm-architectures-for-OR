Here is a clean mathematical optimization model for the problem, followed by how you could solve it in Python.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x_1 \ge 0\): number of doses of pain killer 1  
- \(x_2 \ge 0\): number of doses of pain killer 2  

(If doses must be whole numbers, you would additionally require \(x_1, x_2 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\).)

---

## 2. Objective function

We want to maximize the total amount of medicine delivered to the back.

- Pain killer 1: 0.8 units to the back per dose  
- Pain killer 2: 0.4 units to the back per dose  

So total back medicine is:
\[
\text{Back} = 0.8x_1 + 0.4x_2
\]

**Objective:**
\[
\max \; 0.8x_1 + 0.4x_2
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1. Sleep medicine constraint (at most 8 units)

- Pain killer 1: 0.3 units of sleep medicine per dose  
- Pain killer 2: 0.6 units of sleep medicine per dose  

Total sleep medicine:
\[
0.3x_1 + 0.6x_2 \le 8
\]

### 3.2. Legs medicine constraint (at least 4 units)

- Pain killer 1: 0.5 units to the legs per dose  
- Pain killer 2: 0.7 units to the legs per dose  

Total legs medicine must be at least 4 units:
\[
0.5x_1 + 0.7x_2 \ge 4
\]

### 3.3. Non-negativity (and integrality if needed)

\[
x_1 \ge 0, \quad x_2 \ge 0
\]

Optionally:
\[
x_1, x_2 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Full linear program (LP) model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\max_{x_1, x_2} \quad & 0.8x_1 + 0.4x_2 \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 0.3x_1 + 0.6x_2 \le 8 \quad &\text{(sleep medicine)}\\
& 0.5x_1 + 0.7x_2 \ge 4 \quad &\text{(legs medicine)}\\
& x_1, x_2 \ge 0 \quad &\text{(non-negativity)}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a linear programming problem. Suitable Python libraries include:

- `PuLP` – simple and user-friendly for linear/integer programming.
- `ortools.linear_solver` (Google OR-Tools) – very powerful and fast.
- `scipy.optimize.linprog` – good for continuous LPs (no integer constraints).
- `Pyomo` – more advanced modeling for complex optimization problems.

For a quick implementation, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** is ideal.

---

## 6. Example using PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Define the problem: maximize
prob = pl.LpProblem("Painkiller_Optimization", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables (continuous, >= 0)
x1 = pl.LpVariable("x1", lowBound=0)
x2 = pl.LpVariable("x2", lowBound=0)

# Objective: maximize back medicine: 0.8*x1 + 0.4*x2
prob += 0.8 * x1 + 0.4 * x2

# Constraints
# Sleep medicine: 0.3*x1 + 0.6*x2 <= 8
prob += 0.3 * x1 + 0.6 * x2 <= 8

# Legs medicine: 0.5*x1 + 0.7*x2 >= 4
prob += 0.5 * x1 + 0.7 * x2 >= 4

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Optimal x1 (doses of pain killer 1):", x1.value())
print("Optimal x2 (doses of pain killer 2):", x2.value())
print("Max back medicine:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```
