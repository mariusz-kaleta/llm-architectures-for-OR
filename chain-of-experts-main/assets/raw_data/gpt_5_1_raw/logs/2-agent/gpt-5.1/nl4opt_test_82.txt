[MODELER]:
Here is a clean optimization model for this problem and how to solve it.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x\) = number of bike shifts (integer, \(x \ge 0\))  
- \(y\) = number of scooter shifts (integer, \(y \ge 0\))

---

## 2. Objective function

Maximize total tips:

- Each bike shift: \$50  
- Each scooter shift: \$43  

\[
\text{Maximize } Z = 50x + 43y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

1. **Shift limit** (at most 40 shifts per month):
\[
x + y \le 40
\]

2. **Energy limit** (at most 230 units of energy):
- Bike shift uses 5 energy units  
- Scooter shift uses 6 energy units

\[
5x + 6y \le 230
\]

3. **Order requirement** (must deliver at least 320 orders):
- Bike shift delivers 10 orders  
- Scooter shift delivers 7 orders  

\[
10x + 7y \ge 320
\]

4. **Minimum scooter shifts** (at least 5 scooter shifts):

\[
y \ge 5
\]

5. **Integrality and non-negativity**:
\[
x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

This is a **mixed integer linear programming (MILP)** problem (integer but linear constraints and objective), but here both variables are integers.

---

## 4. Suitable Python libraries

You can model and solve this with any standard MILP solver. Common Python options:

- **PuLP** – very convenient modeling library; works with CBC (included) and others.
- **OR-Tools** (`ortools.linear_solver` or `ortools.math_opt`) – very fast and industrial-grade.
- **Pyomo** – general-purpose optimization modeling.
- **scipy.optimize** – not ideal here because it doesn’t directly support integer variables.

For this problem, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are the simplest choices.

---

## 5. Example in PuLP

```python
import pulp as pl

# Problem
prob = pl.LpProblem("DeliveryShifts", pl.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables (integer)
x = pl.LpVariable("bike_shifts", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")
y = pl.LpVariable("scooter_shifts", lowBound=0, cat="Integer")

# Objective: maximize tips
prob += 50 * x + 43 * y

# Constraints
prob += x + y <= 40                 # shift limit
prob += 5 * x + 6 * y <= 230        # energy limit
prob += 10 * x + 7 * y >= 320       # orders requirement
prob += y >= 5                      # minimum scooter shifts

# Solve
prob.solve(pl.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

print("Status:", pl.LpStatus[prob.status])
print("Bike shifts (x):", pl.value(x))
print("Scooter shifts (y):", pl.value(y))
print("Max tips:", pl.value(prob.objective))
```

This will compute the optimal numbers of bike and scooter shifts to maximize tips while satisfying all constraints.
