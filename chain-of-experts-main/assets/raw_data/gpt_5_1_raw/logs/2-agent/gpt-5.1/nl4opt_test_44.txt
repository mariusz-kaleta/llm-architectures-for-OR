[MODELER]:
Here is a precise optimization model for the problem.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x\) = number of vintage bottles to produce (integer, \(x \geq 0\))  
- \(y\) = number of regular bottles to produce (integer, \(y \geq 0\))

---

## 2. Objective

We want to maximize the total number of bottles produced:

\[
\max \; Z = x + y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

### a) Wine availability (capacity constraint)

Each vintage bottle uses 500 ml; each regular bottle uses 750 ml. Total wine available is 100,000 ml:

\[
500x + 750y \leq 100000
\]

### b) Mix constraint (regular vs vintage)

The number of regular bottles must be at least 4 times the number of vintage bottles:

\[
y \geq 4x
\]

### c) Minimum vintage bottles

At least 10 vintage bottles must be made:

\[
x \geq 10
\]

### d) Non-negativity and integrality

\[
x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\]

---

## 4. Complete mathematical model

\[
\begin{align*}
\max \quad & x + y \\
\text{s.t.} \quad
& 500x + 750y \leq 100000 \\
& y \geq 4x \\
& x \geq 10 \\
& x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
\end{align*}
\]

---

## 5. Solving / interpretation of optimal solution

Use the mix constraint \(y \ge 4x\). To maximize total bottles for fixed \(x\), you want \(y\) as large as possible, but the wine capacity will limit it.  
Try taking the boundary case \(y = 4x\) (since more \(y\) uses more wine per bottle count mix):

Substitute into wine constraint:
\[
500x + 750(4x) \le 100000 \\
500x + 3000x \le 100000 \\
3500x \le 100000 \\
x \le \frac{100000}{3500} \approx 28.57
\]

With integrality and \(x \ge 10\), the largest feasible integer is \(x = 28\). Then:
- \(y \ge 4 \cdot 28 = 112\)
- Check wine use: \(500 \cdot 28 + 750 \cdot 112 = 14000 + 84000 = 98000 \le 100000\)

We can try increasing \(y\) beyond \(4x\). For \(x = 28\):

\[
500 \cdot 28 + 750y \le 100000 \Rightarrow 14000 + 750y \le 100000 \Rightarrow 750y \le 86000 \Rightarrow y \le 114.66
\]

So \(y\) can be at most 114. To maximize \(x+y\), choose the largest feasible \(y\): \(y=114\).

- Feasibility check:  
  - Regular at least 4 times vintage: \(114 \ge 4 \cdot 28 = 112\) ✓  
  - Wine: \(500 \cdot 28 + 750 \cdot 114 = 14000 + 85500 = 99500 \le 100000\) ✓

Total bottles: \(28 + 114 = 142\).

No larger total is possible with other integer \(x\): if \(x\) increases, the capacity constraint becomes tighter; if \(x\) decreases, you lose bottles because the mix constraint forces more regular per vintage and the capacity is fixed.

**Optimal solution:**
- Vintage bottles: \(x^* = 28\)  
- Regular bottles: \(y^* = 114\)  
- Maximum total bottles: \(142\)

---

## 6. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small mixed-integer linear program (MILP). Good libraries:

- **PuLP** (pure Python, easy to use)
- **OR-Tools (Google)** – `ortools.linear_solver` for MILP
- **Pyomo** – more general algebraic modeling language

For a quick and simple implementation, **PuLP** is a very good choice.

[PROGRAMMER-VALIDATOR]:
import pulp

# Define the problem
prob = pulp.LpProblem("Wine_Production", pulp.LpMaximize)

# Decision variables
x = pulp.LpVariable('x', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # vintage
y = pulp.LpVariable('y', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # regular

# Objective function: maximize total bottles
prob += x + y

# Constraints
prob += 500 * x + 750 * y <= 100000      # wine capacity
prob += y >= 4 * x                       # mix constraint
prob += x >= 10                          # minimum vintage

# Solve the problem
prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

# Extract objective value
final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")
