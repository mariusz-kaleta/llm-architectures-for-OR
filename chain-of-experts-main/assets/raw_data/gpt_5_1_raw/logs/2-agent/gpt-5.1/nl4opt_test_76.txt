[MODELER]:
Here is a clean mathematical model of the problem and how to solve it.

---

## 1. Decision variables

Let:

- \(x\) = number of small kegs used  
- \(y\) = number of large kegs used  

Both must be nonnegative integers:

- \(x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}\)

---

## 2. Objective function

Each small keg holds 40 liters, each large keg holds 100 liters.

Maximize total transported water:
\[
\max \; Z = 40x + 100y
\]

---

## 3. Constraints

1. **Availability of kegs**  
   At most 30 small and at most 10 large:
   \[
   x \le 30
   \]
   \[
   y \le 10
   \]

2. **At least twice as many small as large**  
   \[
   x \ge 2y
   \]

3. **Total kegs capacity** (at most 25 total):
   \[
   x + y \le 25
   \]

4. **At least 5 large kegs**:
   \[
   y \ge 5
   \]

5. **Integrality and non-negativity**:
   \[
   x, y \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}
   \]

This is a 2-variable integer linear programming (ILP) problem.

---

## 4. Solving the model logically

Because constraints are small, we can reason directly:

- From \(y \ge 5\) and \(y \le 10\), we have \(y \in \{5,6,7,8,9,10\}\).
- For each feasible \(y\), the constraints on \(x\) are:
  - \(x \le 30\)
  - \(x \ge 2y\)
  - \(x + y \le 25 \Rightarrow x \le 25 - y\)

So:
\[
2y \le x \le \min(30, 25 - y)
\]

We seek to maximize \(40x + 100y\). For fixed \(y\), this increases with \(x\), so choose:
\[
x = \min(30, 25 - y)
\]
subject to \(x \ge 2y\).

Check feasible pairs:

- \(y = 5\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 5 = 20\), and \(x \ge 10\) → choose \(x = 20\)  
  Water = \(40 \cdot 20 + 100 \cdot 5 = 800 + 500 = 1300\)

- \(y = 6\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 6 = 19\), \(x \ge 12\) → choose \(x = 19\)  
  Water = \(40 \cdot 19 + 100 \cdot 6 = 760 + 600 = 1360\)

- \(y = 7\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 7 = 18\), \(x \ge 14\) → choose \(x = 18\)  
  Water = \(40 \cdot 18 + 100 \cdot 7 = 720 + 700 = 1420\)

- \(y = 8\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 8 = 17\), \(x \ge 16\) → choose \(x = 17\)  
  Water = \(40 \cdot 17 + 100 \cdot 8 = 680 + 800 = 1480\)

- \(y = 9\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 9 = 16\), \(x \ge 18\) → infeasible (no \(x\) such that \(18 \le x \le 16\))

- \(y = 10\):  
  \(x \le 25 - 10 = 15\), \(x \ge 20\) → infeasible

Best feasible solution:  
\[
x = 17,\quad y = 8,\quad Z = 1480 \text{ liters}
\]

---

## 5. Final answer

- Number of small kegs: **17**  
- Number of large kegs: **8**  
- Maximum transported water: **1480 liters**

---

## 6. Suitable Python libraries

This is a tiny integer linear program; any standard LP/ILP library will handle it:

- **PuLP** (`pulp`) – very straightforward for educational and small ILP problems.
- **OR-Tools** (`ortools.linear_solver` or `ortools.sat.python.cp_model`) – powerful and efficient.
- **Pyomo** – more general algebraic modeling language for optimization.

For simplicity and readability, PuLP or OR-Tools are ideal here.

import itertools

def objective(x, y):
    return 40 * x + 100 * y

best_val = None
best_solution = None

# Enumerate all feasible integer pairs (x, y)
for x, y in itertools.product(range(0, 31), range(0, 11)):
    # Constraints:
    # 1) x <= 30, y <= 10 are already ensured by ranges
    # 2) x >= 2y
    # 3) x + y <= 25
    # 4) y >= 5
    if x >= 2 * y and x + y <= 25 and y >= 5:
        val = objective(x, y)
        if best_val is None or val > best_val:
            best_val = val
            best_solution = (x, y)

# For completeness, we could print the solution (but requirement is only final objective)
# print("Best (x, y):", best_solution, "Objective:", best_val)

print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {best_val}")
