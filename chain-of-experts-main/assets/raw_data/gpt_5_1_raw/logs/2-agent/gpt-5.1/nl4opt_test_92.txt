[MODELER]:
Let’s turn this into a clear optimization model.

## 1. Decision variables

Let  
- \(x_Z \ge 0\): number of ads on z-tube  
- \(x_S \ge 0\): number of ads on soorchle  
- \(x_W \ge 0\): number of ads on wassa  

These will be **integer** variables in a full model, since you can’t buy a fraction of an ad.

## 2. Objective function (maximize total viewers)

Viewers per ad:
- z-tube: 400,000 viewers/ad
- soorchle: 5,000 viewers/ad
- wassa: 3,000 viewers/ad

Maximize total audience:
\[
\max\; V = 400{,}000\,x_Z + 5{,}000\,x_S + 3{,}000\,x_W
\]

## 3. Constraints

### 3.1 Budget constraint

Costs:
- z-tube: \$1000/ad
- soorchle: \$200/ad
- wassa: \$100/ad

Weekly budget: \$10,000

\[
1000\,x_Z + 200\,x_S + 100\,x_W \le 10{,}000
\]

### 3.2 Soorchle upper limit

Soorchle limits a single company to at most 15 ads:

\[
x_S \le 15
\]

### 3.3 Balance constraints among media

Let total number of ads be:
\[
T = x_Z + x_S + x_W
\]

#### 3.3.1 At most a third of all ads on wassa

“At most a third of the total number of advertisements should occur on wassa”:
\[
x_W \le \frac{1}{3}(x_Z + x_S + x_W)
\]

Multiply by 3:
\[
3x_W \le x_Z + x_S + x_W
\]
\[
2x_W \le x_Z + x_S
\]

This is a linear constraint.

#### 3.3.2 At least 5% of ads on z-tube

“At least 5% should occur on z-tube”:
\[
x_Z \ge 0.05(x_Z + x_S + x_W)
\]

Rewrite:
\[
x_Z \ge 0.05x_Z + 0.05x_S + 0.05x_W
\]
\[
0.95x_Z \ge 0.05x_S + 0.05x_W
\]

Multiply both sides by 20 to clear decimals:
\[
19x_Z \ge x_S + x_W
\]

Again a linear constraint.

### 3.4 Nonnegativity and integrality

\[
x_Z, x_S, x_W \ge 0, \quad x_Z, x_S, x_W \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

(If you treat them as continuous variables, this becomes a linear program; but integer is more realistic.)

## 4. Full mathematical model

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Maximize}\quad & 400{,}000\,x_Z + 5{,}000\,x_S + 3{,}000\,x_W\\[4pt]
\text{subject to}\quad
& 1000\,x_Z + 200\,x_S + 100\,x_W \le 10{,}000 \quad\text{(budget)}\\
& x_S \le 15 \quad\text{(soorchle limit)}\\
& 2x_W \le x_Z + x_S \quad\text{(wassa ≤ 1/3 of ads)}\\
& 19x_Z \ge x_S + x_W \quad\text{(z-tube ≥ 5% of ads)}\\
& x_Z, x_S, x_W \ge 0,\; x_Z,x_S,x_W \in \mathbb{Z}
\end{aligned}
\]

## 5. Suitable Python libraries

This is a small integer linear programming (ILP) problem. Good Python options:

- **PuLP**: Very simple modeling interface; can use CBC solver (bundled).  
- **OR-Tools (Google)**: `ortools.linear_solver` or `ortools.sat.python.cp_model` for integer linear constraints.  
- **Pyomo**: More general algebraic modeling, good for larger or more complex models.  

For this problem, **PuLP** or **OR-Tools** are ideal due to simplicity.

import pulp

def main():
    # Create the problem: Maximize viewers
    prob = pulp.LpProblem("Ad_Allocation", pulp.LpMaximize)

    # Decision variables (non-negative integers)
    x_Z = pulp.LpVariable('x_Z', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # z-tube
    x_S = pulp.LpVariable('x_S', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # soorchle
    x_W = pulp.LpVariable('x_W', lowBound=0, cat='Integer')  # wassa

    # Objective function: maximize total viewers
    prob += 400000 * x_Z + 5000 * x_S + 3000 * x_W

    # Constraints

    # Budget: 1000 x_Z + 200 x_S + 100 x_W <= 10000
    prob += 1000 * x_Z + 200 * x_S + 100 * x_W <= 10000

    # Soorchle limit: x_S <= 15
    prob += x_S <= 15

    # Wassa at most a third of total: 2 x_W <= x_Z + x_S
    prob += 2 * x_W <= x_Z + x_S

    # Z-tube at least 5% of total: 19 x_Z >= x_S + x_W
    prob += 19 * x_Z >= x_S + x_W

    # Solve
    prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=False))

    # Compute final objective value (total viewers)
    final_obj = pulp.value(prob.objective)

    # Print FINAL_OBJ line as required
    print(f"FINAL_OBJ: {final_obj}")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    main()
